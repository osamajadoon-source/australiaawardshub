What is the Forrest Research Foundation PhD Scholarship?

The Forrest Research Foundation PhD Scholarship is one of Australia's most prestigious and generously funded PhD awards. Established through a AUD $65 million donation by Andrew and Nicola Forrest (Minderoo Foundation), it attracts world-leading doctoral researchers to Western Australia's five partner universities: UWA, Curtin, ECU, Murdoch and the University of Notre Dame Australia.

The full scholarship package is valued at over AUD $270,000 over four years — one of the largest PhD scholarship packages offered by any Australian institution. It covers full tuition, a competitive stipend, accommodation at the purpose-built Forrest Hall on the UWA campus, and a research travel allowance. Open to all nationalities and all disciplines.

Full scholarship package breakdown

📈

Stipend (living allowance)

AUD $34,315–$37,500/yr

Rate determined by the enrolling university, indexed annually. Paid fortnightly. Tax-exempt. Equivalent to or exceeding the national RTP stipend rate.

🏠

Accommodation at Forrest Hall

AUD $25,740/yr

Luxury self-contained studio apartment at Forrest Hall, UWA Perth campus. Scholars live in a multidisciplinary research community. Exemptions considered for scholars with dependants.

✈️

Research & travel allowance

AUD $12,000

One-off allowance for approved research expenses, conference travel and overseas research visits. Plus economy airfare to Perth for the scholar and immediate dependants.

🎓

Full tuition (international)

100% covered

International Forrest Scholars receive an RTP Fees Offset Scholarship or equivalent covering full international tuition fees plus Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the scholarship duration.

🌟 AUD $270,000+ over four years

The total Forrest Scholarship package over a standard 4-year PhD — stipend ($37,500 × 4) + accommodation ($25,740 × 4) + travel ($12,000) + full tuition — exceeds AUD $270,000. This places it among the most generous PhD scholarships offered by any Australian institution, comfortably above the national RTP stipend benchmark.

You apply to the Forrest Research Foundation and simultaneously apply to your chosen university's PhD program. The scholarship is assessed centrally by the Foundation — your choice of university does not affect your chances of receiving the scholarship.

Eligibility requirements

  • Academic excellence: Most Forrest Scholars have a GPA of 3.80/4.0 or above (First Class Honours or international equivalent). This is the most competitive eligibility factor — applicants below this threshold are rarely successful
  • Citizenship: Open to all nationalities — both domestic and international applicants
  • Disciplines: All academic disciplines are eligible. Forrest Scholars have come from engineering, medicine, humanities, social sciences, marine biology, economics and computer science
  • Enrolment: Must be enrolling in a PhD at one of the five WA partner universities. Not available for Masters by Research or MPhil
  • New PhD candidates only: Must not have previously enrolled in the proposed PhD or undertaken research towards a research doctorate
  • Residency: Scholars are expected to reside at Forrest Hall for the duration of their scholarship (exemptions considered case by case for scholars with dependants or exceptional circumstances)
  • Full-time internal: Must be enrolled full-time and on-campus at the WA university

How to apply — step by step

1

Identify a supervisor and confirm their support

Contact 5–8 potential supervisors at your preferred WA university. Read their recent papers before reaching out. Secure a written letter of support — this is required for your application. Start this 6–8 months before the Forrest application round opens.

2

Apply for PhD admission at your chosen university

Simultaneously submit your PhD application to the target university (UWA, Curtin, ECU, Murdoch or Notre Dame). The Forrest Foundation requires confirmation of your university admission application when you submit to them.

3

Apply to the Forrest Research Foundation

Submit your Forrest Scholarship application at forrestresearch.org.au during the application window (next round opens July 2026). Include your research proposal, supervisor letter, academic transcripts and CV. The Foundation assesses applicants on research potential, not just GPA.

4

Interview and selection

Shortlisted candidates are invited to interview with the Forrest Research Foundation Governors. The Foundation assesses each applicant on research quality, ambition, character and the potential to make a positive difference in the world.

5

Commence PhD and move to Forrest Hall

Successful Forrest Scholars receive an offer from both the Foundation and their enrolling university. They relocate to Perth (relocation economy airfare covered), take up residence at Forrest Hall and begin a unique researcher development programme alongside their PhD.

Next application round — July 2026

The Forrest Research Foundation opens its annual application round in July each year for scholars commencing the following year. The 2026 round has closed — the 2027 round will open approximately July 2026. Subscribe to updates at forrestresearch.org.au. Start identifying supervisors now — the process from first contact to confirmed supervisor takes 3–6 months at competitive universities.

Tips to strengthen your Forrest application

01

Articulate world-changing research potential

The Foundation explicitly looks for scholars whose "research has the potential to change the world." Your proposal must go beyond technical competence — explain the real-world impact of your proposed PhD, not just its academic contribution.

02

Start supervisor outreach 6 months early

The WA university system is smaller than Sydney/Melbourne. Top supervisors at UWA and Curtin in competitive fields fill their PhD groups early. Contact supervisors in January–February for a July Foundation application.

03

Apply to only one university

The Foundation explicitly states that multiple applications are not considered. Choose your target university based on research fit, not ranking. A great supervisor at Murdoch is worth more than a weak supervisor at UWA.

04

Prepare for the Governor interview

Shortlisted scholars attend an interview with the Foundation Governors. Prepare to discuss your research clearly for a non-specialist audience, explain your career trajectory, and articulate why you are the right person for this scholarship — not just why you deserve it academically.
