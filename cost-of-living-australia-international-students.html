Australia Awards scholars in Sydney and Melbourne — budgeting tips

If you are an Australia Awards scholar placed at a Sydney or Melbourne university, the stipend is liveable but requires discipline. Key strategies: choose accommodation in outer suburbs with good public transport (Parramatta for UNSW/Sydney, Clayton for Monash); cook at home using Aldi, Woolworths Essentials or IGA; use the Opal/Myki weekly fare cap; and take advantage of student discounts on entertainment, gym memberships and software.

How to reduce your living costs in Australia

  • Live in share housing, not on-campus: On-campus accommodation is convenient but costs AUD 200–400/month more than equivalent share housing within 20 minutes of campus. Use Facebook groups, Flatmates.com.au or student noticeboard apps to find share houses
  • Cook at home and shop at Aldi: Aldi is significantly cheaper than Coles and Woolworths. A weekly shop for one person costs AUD 60–90 at Aldi versus AUD 90–140 at the major supermarkets. Student-heavy suburbs have Aldi stores within walking distance of most major campuses
  • Use weekly transport caps: Sydney Opal and Melbourne Myki both have weekly fare caps (around AUD 50–55) after which you travel free for the rest of the week. This dramatically reduces transport costs for students who travel daily
  • Maximise student discounts: Australian universities offer concession-rate cinema tickets, gym memberships, software (Adobe, Microsoft) and streaming services. Always carry your student ID and ask for the student rate before paying full price
  • Apply for a Tax File Number (TFN) immediately on arrival: If you work part-time (international students are permitted to work up to 48 hours per fortnight), a TFN ensures you are not taxed at the emergency rate. Apply online at ato.gov.au within the first week of arrival
