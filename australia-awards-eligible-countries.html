Which countries can apply for Australia Awards? The full list of ~50 eligible partner countries, organised by region — with the managing Australian Embassy or High Commission and annual application deadline for each.

🌎 50+ eligible countries — updated 2026

Australia Awards are administered by DFAT. The list of eligible countries reflects Australia’s bilateral development partnerships and is updated periodically. Citizens of any country on this list may apply — however, each country has its own priority sectors, annual allocation, and application timeline managed by the Australian Embassy or High Commission in that country.

India, China and Brazil are NOT eligible for Australia Awards

India, China, Brazil and most upper-middle-income countries are not on the Australia Awards eligible list — the program is targeted at lower and lower-middle income countries. Citizens of these countries can still access Australian university research scholarships (Melbourne MGRS, Sydney USYDIS, Monash MGS, RTP) which are open to all nationalities. See our India scholarships guide for alternatives.

How eligibility is determined

DFAT determines eligible countries based on Australia’s Official Development Assistance (ODA) framework — specifically, countries where Australia has an active bilateral development partnership. Countries are categorised into priority tiers that influence how many scholarship places are allocated annually. Being on the eligible list does not guarantee a scholarship — it means citizens of that country may apply. Actual allocation depends on your country’s annual quota, the quality of applications received, and how well proposed fields of study align with DFAT’s bilateral priorities for that country.

Frequently asked questions

Check the DFAT Australia Awards website at dfat.gov.au/australiaawards. The full list of eligible countries is published there and updated when changes occur. The annual application portal (OASIS) at australiaawards.gov.au also lists eligible countries at registration. If your country is not on the list, you are not eligible for the Australia Awards government scholarship but may still apply for university research and merit scholarships which are open to all nationalities.
