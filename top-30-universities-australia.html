How to use this ranking

Rankings tell you about research output, employer reputation, and faculty quality — but not which university is the best fit for your situation. Before choosing based solely on rank, consider four factors that rankings don't capture: scholarship availability at your target institution, the strength of your specific discipline at that university, the city you want to live in, and the cost of living you can sustain.

The top 8 universities — the Group of Eight — offer the strongest research scholarships for international students. If you are applying for a PhD or Masters by Research, your search should start with Go8 institutions. If you are a coursework student, universities ranked 9–18 often offer comparable education in your field with more accessible scholarship programs and lower competition.

How this ranking works

AA Hub Rankings methodology: Our combined score synthesises data from QS World University Rankings 2026, Times Higher Education 2026, and ARWU 2024 — weighted to reflect their relevance to international students. The score is presented out of 100, with the University of Melbourne as the benchmark at 98. Rankings are reviewed annually as new global data is released.

Group of Eight (Go8): Australia's eight leading research universities — Melbourne, Sydney, UNSW, ANU, Monash, UQ, UWA, and Adelaide — receive the majority of Australia's research funding and produce most of its internationally-cited research. They offer the widest range of fully funded scholarships for international students.

These rankings change every year. QS 2026 was released in April 2026; THE 2026 in October 2025; ARWU 2024 in August 2024. Always check the official ranking websites for the latest figures before making study decisions.
