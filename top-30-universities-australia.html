How this ranking works

Combined score methodology: Our combined score weights QS (40%), THE (35%), and ARWU (25%) to reflect their relative influence in the international student market. QS and THE are the most widely referenced by Australian universities themselves; ARWU carries significant weight in research circles but focuses exclusively on research output, which can underrepresent universities with strong teaching or industry programs.

What "~" means: The tilde (~) indicates an approximate global rank where the exact figure wasn't available in our sources. Band rankings (e.g., "101-150") reflect how ARWU publishes results for positions beyond 100.

Group of Eight (Go8): Australia's eight leading research universities — Melbourne, Sydney, UNSW, ANU, Monash, UQ, UWA, and Adelaide — receive the majority of Australia's research funding and produce most of its internationally-cited research. They offer the widest range of fully funded scholarships for international students.

These rankings change every year. QS 2026 was released in April 2026; THE 2026 in October 2025; ARWU 2024 in August 2024. Always check the official ranking websites for the latest figures before making study decisions.
