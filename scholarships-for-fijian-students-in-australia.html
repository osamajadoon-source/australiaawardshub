Scholarships in Australia for Fijian Students 2026/2027

Fiji is a priority Pacific partner country for Australia Awards and one of Australia's closest regional relationships. Fijian citizens have access to fully-funded government scholarships, university research packages, and Pacific-specific education initiatives — with dedicated in-country support from the Australian High Commission in Suva and a strong network of Fijian alumni across Australian universities.

Scholarships open to Fijian students

Fijian citizens have access to a range of Australian scholarship pathways. The flagship option is the Australia Awards Scholarship — fully funded, covering tuition, living allowance, airfare, and health insurance. Fijian students are also eligible for major university research scholarships providing AUD 37,000–42,754 per year, and for the Destination Australia regional scholarship.

🌊 Fiji — a high-priority Pacific partner

As a Pacific island nation facing significant climate change and development challenges, Fiji receives targeted Australia Awards support. Climate resilience, governance, and economic diversification are consistently among the top-funded fields for Fijian scholars. The Australia-Fiji bilateral relationship includes dedicated support structures that make the scholarship journey particularly well-supported compared to many other partner countries.

Australia Awards Scholarships 2027

Full funding Masters · PhD Pacific priority country

Fiji's flagship scholarship pathway, managed by the Australian High Commission in Suva. Covers full tuition at any participating Australian university, return economy class airfare (Suva/Nadi to Australia), a Contribution to Living Expenses (CLE) of AUD 29,710+ per year, OSHC health cover, and an Introductory Academic Program (IAP) orientation in Australia.

  • Annual deadline: 30 April via the OASIS portal at australiaawards.gov.au
  • Managed locally by the Australian High Commission Suva
  • English language exemption may apply — Fiji is English-medium; confirm eligibility with the AHC early
  • Two-year home return condition applies — scholars must return to Fiji for at least two continuous years after completing their scholarship
  • The DILP (Development Impact and Linkages Plan) is the most critical document in your application

Destination Australia — Regional Campuses

AUD 15,000/year All study levels All nationalities

Fijian students studying at regional Australian campuses can access AUD 15,000/year through Destination Australia, on top of any other scholarship. Regional study also provides an extra 1–2 years on the post-study work visa — useful for Fijian students planning to gain Australian work experience. James Cook University (JCU) in Townsville and Cairns is an excellent option for Fijian students in marine science, environmental management, and tourism.

Fiji's priority sectors for Australia Awards 2026

DFAT determines priority sectors for Fiji annually. As a Pacific island nation particularly vulnerable to climate change and economic volatility, Fiji's priorities reflect both national development needs and the regional Australia-Pacific partnership agenda. Current priority areas include:

  • Climate change and disaster resilience: Climate adaptation, disaster risk reduction, environmental science, marine science, coastal management — Fiji's highest-priority area given its frontline climate vulnerability
  • Governance and public administration: Public sector management, financial accountability, public law, anti-corruption, intergovernmental relations
  • Health systems and public health: Public health management, epidemiology, health policy, tropical and communicable disease
  • Education quality and access: Education policy, teacher training, curriculum development, vocational education
  • Economic development and trade: Economics, trade policy, development finance, tourism and hospitality management
  • Infrastructure: Civil engineering, water and sanitation, renewable energy, transport

Climate change — Fiji's flagship priority

DFAT has consistently identified climate resilience as Fiji's leading scholarship priority. Fijian applicants proposing study in climate science, environmental policy, disaster risk reduction, marine ecology, or renewable energy are particularly well-positioned — provided their DILP clearly articulates how their proposed study will translate into measurable impact in Fiji's specific climate context.

English language requirements for Fijian applicants

Fiji uses English as the primary medium of instruction at secondary and tertiary level. Fijian applicants may be eligible for an English language exemption from the standard IELTS requirement if they completed all secondary and tertiary education in English.

  • Contact the Australian High Commission Suva early to confirm whether your educational background qualifies for the exemption
  • If an IELTS score is required, the standard is IELTS Academic 6.5 overall, no band below 6.0
  • If your score falls short of the requirement, Australia Awards may fund Pre-Course English (PCE) training in Australia before your degree commences
  • The University of the South Pacific (USP), headquartered in Suva, is widely recognised by Australian universities as an English-medium institution

What Fijian applicants need to prepare

  • Academic transcripts from all universities attended — certified official copies from USP, Fiji National University (FNU), or other institutions attended. Allow 2–3 weeks to obtain certified copies
  • Employment verification letter — confirming your role, responsibilities, and duration (minimum 2 years post-bachelor's for Australia Awards postgraduate applications)
  • DILP — Development Impact and Linkages Plan (Parts 1 and 2) — begin in February, allow 3–4 weeks for drafting and revision
  • Two referee reports — DFAT referee templates available on the OASIS portal. Give referees at least 4 weeks
  • Police clearance — from the Fiji Police Force. Allow 2–3 weeks processing time
  • IELTS score — if not eligible for English exemption
  • Health examination — required for the student visa, completed at an approved panel physician after receiving your scholarship offer

Australia Awards 2027 — application timeline for Fijian students

January — Contact AHC and confirm English requirement

Contact the Australian High Commission Suva to confirm whether you qualify for an English language exemption. Attend any pre-application information sessions the AHC organises — these are valuable for understanding the current priority areas and common mistakes.

January–February — Book IELTS if needed and download country profile

If an IELTS score is required, book for January or February to allow time for a resit. Download Fiji's current DFAT country profile from dfat.gov.au to confirm priority areas for this cycle.

February — Register on OASIS and start your DILP

Register at australiaawards.gov.au as soon as the portal opens. Begin your DILP immediately — this is the document that wins or loses applications. Allow 3–4 weeks for drafting and revision.

March — Collect documents and contact referees

Obtain certified transcripts, employment letter, and Fiji Police Force clearance. Contact your two referees and give them at least 4 weeks to complete DFAT referee reports.

April — Final review and early submission

Submit your complete application at least one week before 30 April 14:00 AEST. The OASIS system experiences heavy traffic and technical issues in the final days — early submission is strongly advised.

June–August — Shortlisting and interviews

Shortlisted candidates are contacted by the Australian High Commission Suva and invited to in-country interviews, typically held in Suva.

October–December — Outcomes and offer

Final outcomes announced. Successful scholars begin pre-departure briefings and any English preparation coordinated by the AHC and their Australian university's scholarship manager.

Best cities and universities for Fijian students in Australia

Brisbane / Queensland

University of Queensland is particularly strong for marine science, public health, and environmental science — fields closely aligned with Fiji's priorities. Warmer climate, direct flights from Nadi. Growing Pacific community.

Townsville (JCU)

James Cook University — world-leading for marine science, tropical ecology, and climate adaptation. Particularly well-suited for Fijian students in environmental fields. Regional campus means AUD 15,000/yr Destination Australia and extra post-study work visa time.

Canberra (ANU)

Australian National University — #1 in Australia (QS). Strong for public policy, governance, international relations, and climate policy. Active Pacific student community. Direct link to Canberra's policy and government sector.

Melbourne

University of Melbourne leads in law, education, and public health. Large, diverse international community. Well-connected Pacific student associations. Cooler climate — factor this in when packing.

How to strengthen your DILP as a Fijian applicant

  • Anchor your DILP in Fiji's national development plans. Reference the Fiji National Development Plan and DFAT's Indo-Pacific strategy. Assessors look for applicants who understand the bilateral context, not just their personal career goals
  • Make climate change central if it's relevant to your field. Climate resilience is DFAT's highest priority for Fiji. Even if your field is governance or health, demonstrating how your proposed study intersects with climate adaptation strengthens your application
  • Name a specific organisation for your return role. The Fiji Government, Fiji Revenue and Customs Service, Fiji National University, Ministry of Health, or civil society organisations all count. Generic plans score poorly
  • Attend the AHC's information sessions. The Australian High Commission in Suva typically holds pre-application sessions in January–February. These provide specific guidance on the current priority areas and what assessors are looking for this cycle

⚠️ Two-year home return condition

All Australia Awards scholars, including Fijians, must return to Fiji and reside there for at least two continuous years after completing their scholarship before applying for any other Australian visa — including skilled migration, temporary graduate, or employer-sponsored visas. The period begins from your scholarship completion date, not your departure from Australia. Violation results in a debt equal to the full cost of your scholarship.
