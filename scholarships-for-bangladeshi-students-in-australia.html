Scholarships in Australia for Bangladeshi Students 2026/2027

Bangladesh is a priority participating country in Australia's flagship scholarship program — the Australia Awards — making it one of the strongest national positions from which to apply for a fully funded Australian education. Bangladeshi students also have access to university merit scholarships, research funding packages, and region-specific awards worth up to AUD 42,754 per year. This guide covers every scholarship available to Bangladeshi citizens in 2026 and 2027, with eligibility criteria, required documents, and a step-by-step application timeline.

Documents

What Bangladeshi applicants need to prepare

Australian scholarship applications require careful document preparation — particularly for Bangladeshi students, whose transcripts are often in Bengali and require certified translation. Begin collecting these documents at least 3–4 months before your target deadline.

✓
Valid Bangladeshi passport
Must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your intended travel date. Machine-readable passport required for the visa application. Apply for renewal at the Bangladesh Passport Office well in advance.
✓
IELTS / PTE score
Australia Awards requires IELTS Academic 6.5 (no band below 6.0). Book early — IELTS centres in Dhaka and Chittagong fill up quickly. Aim for a test sitting at least 3 months before the application deadline to allow time for a resit.
✓
Academic transcripts (English translated)
If your transcripts are in Bengali, they must be officially translated into English by a NAATI-certified translator and notarised. Allow 2–3 weeks for translation services in Dhaka.
✓
Degree certificates (English translated)
Same translation and notarisation requirement as transcripts. Both your bachelor's and any postgraduate degree certificates must be included.
✓
Employment verification letter
On official letterhead from your employer confirming your job title, responsibilities, and duration of employment. Must be signed by an authorised person. Australia Awards requires 2+ years of post-bachelor's work experience.
✓
Two referee reports
At least one academic referee is required. Choose referees who can speak specifically to your leadership potential, academic ability, and development-sector work. Give them at least 4 weeks notice.
✓
Statement of purpose / personal statement
A 500–1,000 word statement covering your academic background, why you have chosen this program, and your plans after returning to Bangladesh. For Australia Awards, this must be paired with a strong DILP.
✓
Development Impact and Linkages Plan (DILP)
The most critical document in your Australia Awards application. Must articulate specifically how your Australian qualification will address Bangladesh's development priorities upon your return. Vague plans are the most common reason for rejection.
✓
Police clearance certificate
Required for the visa application. Obtain from the Bangladesh Police Headquarters or the Special Branch. Allow 3–4 weeks processing time.
✓
CV / resume
Highlighting leadership roles, development-sector work, community activities, and academic achievements. For Australia Awards, emphasise roles connected to Bangladesh's national development priorities.
Timeline

Australia Awards 2027 — application timeline for Bangladeshi students

The Australia Awards application window for Bangladesh typically opens in February and closes 30 April. Applications are submitted online via OASIS and managed by the Australian High Commission in Dhaka. Here is how to plan your preparation.

Jan
January 2026
Research, shortlist and book IELTS
Confirm your field aligns with Bangladesh's current priority areas on the DFAT Bangladesh country profile. Book your IELTS test — IELTS centres in Dhaka fill fast. Aim for a test sitting in late January or February to allow time for a resit before the April deadline.
Feb
February 2026
OASIS portal opens — register and begin DILP
The OASIS application portal typically opens in February. Register your account early and begin your profile. Start drafting your DILP immediately — this is the most time-consuming and consequential document in your application. A weak DILP is the most common reason Bangladeshi applicants are rejected.
Mar
March 2026
Documents, translation and referees
Commission certified English translations of your Bengali transcripts and certificates. Contact your referees and provide them at least 4 weeks to complete their reports. Obtain an employment verification letter from your organisation. Draft and refine your personal statement.
Apr
April 2026 — Deadline: 30 April
Final review and submit
Review every section of your application carefully. Ensure your DILP specifically names the development challenge you will address, the role you plan to return to, and the measurable impact you expect to create. Submit at least one week early — OASIS experiences heavy traffic in the final days of the window.
Jun
June–August 2026
Shortlisting and interviews in Dhaka
Shortlisted candidates are contacted for interviews held at the Australian High Commission in Dhaka. Prepare to clearly explain your DILP, your leadership experience, and your return plan. Interviewers are looking for credibility and genuine commitment to development impact in Bangladesh.
Oct
October–December 2026
Outcome and visa application
Final outcomes are announced between October and December. Successful scholars receive a formal offer and begin the student visa (Subclass 500) application. Pre-departure briefings and English training are arranged by the Australian High Commission. Study typically commences February or July 2027.

Application tips

How to maximise your chances as a Bangladeshi applicant

Practical advice for Bangladeshi students applying for Australia Awards and university scholarships in 2026 and 2027.

01

Australia Awards is your #1 priority — apply every cycle

Bangladesh is a priority country — DFAT actively allocates a meaningful number of scholarships to Bangladeshi scholars each year. Many successful recipients applied 2–3 times before winning. If you are shortlisted but not selected, use the feedback to improve your DILP and apply again the following year. The investment is worth it — it is fully funded.

02

Climate change is Bangladesh's strongest card

Bangladesh is one of the world's most climate-vulnerable countries — and this is internationally recognised. If your background is in environmental engineering, disaster risk reduction, WASH, climate adaptation, or coastal management, you are extremely well-positioned for Australia Awards. Frame your DILP around Bangladesh's specific climate vulnerabilities and your concrete plan to address them after returning.

03

The DILP makes or breaks your application

The Development Impact and Linkages Plan is the single most important document in your Australia Awards application. It must be specific — naming the organisation you will return to, the role you will hold, the development challenge you will address, and the measurable outcomes you expect. Generic statements like "improve healthcare in Bangladesh" are consistently rejected. Our DILP guide has real examples from successful applications.

04

Get your Bengali transcripts translated early

Bengali-language transcripts and certificates must be officially translated into English by a NAATI-certified translator and notarised before submission. This process typically takes 2–3 weeks in Dhaka. Commission translations in January or February — do not leave it until March. Poor-quality translations are a common administrative reason for application problems.

05

BUET and DU degrees are well-respected in Australia

Degrees from BUET, University of Dhaka, BRAC University, NSU, KUET, RUET, and other recognised Bangladeshi universities are accepted by all major Australian universities. If you graduated with a First Class (typically 75%+ or 3.5+ GPA), you are academically competitive for most scholarships. For research scholarships, a strong academic record from any recognised institution is the foundation.

06

Apply for research scholarships alongside Australia Awards

You can apply for Australia Awards and a university research scholarship (MGRS, USYDIS, Monash MGS) simultaneously. Much of the preparation overlaps — research proposal, personal statement, and referees serve both applications. If you receive an Australia Awards offer, simply decline the university scholarship. Casting a wider net maximises your chances of getting to Australia.
