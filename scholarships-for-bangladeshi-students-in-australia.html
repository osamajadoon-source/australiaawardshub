Application tips

How to maximise your chances as a Bangladeshi applicant

Practical advice tailored to Bangladeshi students applying for Australian scholarships in 2026/2027.

01

Australia Awards is your #1 priority

Bangladesh is a priority country — DFAT actively allocates scholarships for Bangladeshi scholars. Apply every year until successful — many recipients applied 2-3 times before winning.

02

Priority sectors give you an edge

Bangladesh priority areas: health systems, climate change adaptation, water and sanitation, education quality, and governance. Civil/environmental engineering, public health, education, and economics applicants are well-positioned.

03

English is the most common barrier

IELTS 6.5 overall (no band below 6.0) is required. Many Bangladeshi applicants struggle with Writing and Speaking bands. Start IELTS preparation 6 months before your target deadline and take the test 2-3 times if needed.

04

BUET, DU, BRAC, and NSU degrees are accepted

Degrees from BUET, Dhaka University, BRAC University, NSU, and other recognised Bangladeshi universities are accepted by Australian universities. Bengali-language transcripts should be officially translated and notarised.

05

Apply for research scholarships alongside Australia Awards

Bangladeshi students can apply for Australia Awards AND a university research scholarship simultaneously. Preparation overlaps significantly — the research proposal, personal statement, and references serve both applications.
