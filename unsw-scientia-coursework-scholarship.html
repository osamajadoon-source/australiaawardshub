What the UNSW Scientia Scholarship covers

The UNSW International Scientia Coursework Scholarship is a merit-based partial tuition scholarship for international students enrolling in coursework programs at UNSW Sydney. It is one of Australia's most accessible scholarships because it requires no separate application — eligibility is assessed automatically when UNSW reviews your admissions file.

How to get it

Automatic

No essays, interviews or separate forms. Submit your admissions application and UNSW includes the scholarship in your offer if you qualify.

⚠️ This is a tuition reduction — not a living stipend

The Scientia Scholarship reduces your tuition fees only. It does not cover accommodation, living expenses, airfare, or OSHC health insurance. Budget these separately — Sydney living costs are approximately AUD 22,000–28,000 per year for a single student.

Eligibility requirements

The scholarship is open to international students across all UNSW faculties. Core criteria:

  • Citizenship: International students only — not Australian citizens or permanent residents
  • Study level: Masters by coursework, Graduate Diploma, Graduate Certificate. Not available for PhD or Masters by Research
  • Academic merit: Distinction average (75%+) or international equivalent (~GPA 3.5/4.0). Threshold varies by faculty
  • Enrolled program: Fee-paying UNSW coursework program. Not applicable if you hold an Australia Awards Scholarship (which covers full tuition)
  • English proficiency: Standard UNSW IELTS requirements apply (typically 6.5+) — assessed as part of admission, not separately for the scholarship

How to apply — step by step

1

Apply for admission at UNSW

Submit your application at apply.unsw.edu.au. Upload certified academic transcripts, English language test results, CV and program-specific documents. Your Scientia eligibility is assessed from this same file — no separate scholarship form exists.

2

Receive your offer letter

If you meet the Scientia threshold for your faculty, your conditional or unconditional offer will include the scholarship amount. Not all offers include it — whether you receive it depends on your academic profile relative to the cohort.

3

Accept your offer and pay deposit

Accept through the UNSW portal and pay the required enrolment deposit. The scholarship is confirmed upon acceptance. Tuition reduction is applied automatically to each semester fee invoice.

4

Apply for your Student Visa (Subclass 500)

Use your UNSW offer letter and Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) to apply via ImmiAccount. Your CoE reflects the discounted tuition fee for OSHC cost calculations.

5

Maintain satisfactory academic progress

The scholarship continues each semester you maintain satisfactory academic progress (SAP). Failing or withdrawing from courses may affect continuation. Review UNSW's SAP policy before enrolling.

Consider a research degree for full funding

If your field allows a Masters by Research or PhD pathway, you may qualify for full funding — tuition plus a living stipend — through the Melbourne MGRS, Sydney USYDIS, or Monash MGS. A research degree takes longer but the financial package is dramatically better than any partial coursework award.

6 tips to maximise your Scientia chances

01

Submit certified transcripts — not photocopies

UNSW assesses the scholarship from your submitted transcripts. Uncertified copies can delay or prevent assessment. Submit officially certified originals.

02

Apply early — scholarship pools fill each intake

Scholarships are assessed on a rolling basis. Applying in the first month of the application window significantly improves your chances over late applicants.

03

Target the faculty where your GPA is most competitive

If your average is borderline, consider whether an equivalent program in a less competitive UNSW faculty might cross the threshold more reliably.

04

Apply before IELTS results arrive

UNSW accepts applications without English scores — you can add them later. Apply immediately and add your IELTS result when available. Don't wait.

05

Contact UNSW International Office for guidance

The UNSW International Student Services team can confirm whether your academic background is competitive for your target faculty before you submit.

06

Apply to multiple universities simultaneously

The Scientia is competitive and not guaranteed. Apply to Monash, ANU and other Australian universities at the same time — the document preparation largely overlaps.
