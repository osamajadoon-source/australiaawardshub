UNSW International Scientia Coursework Scholarship

The UNSW International Scientia Coursework Scholarship is one of Australia's most prestigious and generous coursework scholarships, offering either a full tuition waiver or AUD 20,000 per year toward tuition. It is highly competitive and recognises exceptional academic achievement and leadership potential.

Overview

UNSW Sydney is ranked #19 in the world (QS 2025) and is particularly renowned for engineering, law, business (UNSW Business School / AGSM), medicine, and science. The Scientia scholarship program is UNSW's flagship merit award, named after the university's motto. It is one of the few Australian scholarships that can fully fund an international coursework Master's degree.

What does the scholarship cover?

  • Full tuition fee waiver for the minimum program duration (Tier 1)
  • OR AUD 20,000 per year toward tuition fees (Tier 2)
  • Available for eligible undergraduate and postgraduate coursework programs
  • Strong alumni network and industry connections through UNSW

Eligibility requirements

You must be an international student applying to or commencing an eligible coursework program at UNSW. You must demonstrate exceptional academic achievement — typically a WAM of 80+ (Distinction average) or equivalent. Leadership potential and extracurricular achievements are also considered. A separate scholarship application is required (unlike some automatic scholarships at other universities).

Important — check official requirements

Scholarship conditions, values, and deadlines change annually. Always verify the current requirements on the official provider website before applying. Use the "Apply officially" button above to access the authoritative source.

How to apply

Apply for admission to UNSW and simultaneously submit a scholarship application through the UNSW scholarships portal. You will need to provide: academic transcripts, a personal statement, referee reports, English language test results, and evidence of any awards, publications or leadership achievements. Applications are assessed competitively — the earlier you apply, the better.

Frequently asked questions

Both are world-class universities ranked in the global top 50. UNSW is stronger in engineering, law, and business; Sydney is stronger in arts, medicine, and certain sciences. The choice should be based on your specific program, supervisor (for research), and scholarship offer.

UNSW's main campus (Kensington) is approximately 7km from Sydney CBD, near the eastern suburbs beaches. It is well connected by bus and light rail.

Most undergraduate and postgraduate programs are eligible, but check the current list on the UNSW scholarships website — some programs may be excluded. Engineering, Business, IT, and Science programs are typically included.

Scholarship shortlisting may involve an interview in some cases. Check the current round's process on the UNSW scholarships page.

If you hold an Australia Awards Scholarship (which covers tuition), you would not typically receive the Scientia tuition waiver as well. Combining scholarships from different sources (e.g., your home government + Scientia) may be possible — check with UNSW.
