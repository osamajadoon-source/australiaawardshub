Sydney Vice-Chancellor's International Scholarship (VCIS)

The University of Sydney Vice-Chancellor's International Scholarship (VCIS) is an automatic merit-based award for outstanding international students commencing undergraduate or postgraduate coursework study. Students are considered automatically when they receive an offer of admission — no separate application is required.

Provider
University of Sydney
Study level
Undergraduate / Postgraduate Coursework
Award value
AUD 5,000 – AUD 40,000 (tiered by academic merit)
Duration
Applied in Year 1 automatically — renewable subject to academic performance
Eligibility
High-achieving international students receiving an offer from University of Sydney
Application deadline
Considered automatically with your admission offer

What does the scholarship cover?

  • AUD 5,000 to AUD 40,000 scholarship (applied to tuition fees)
  • Tiered award based on academic excellence — higher grades, higher award
  • No separate application needed — automatically assessed with admission
  • Combined with other University of Sydney support programs

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to apply separately?+
No — the VCIS is automatically assessed when you apply for admission. Your scholarship tier is determined by your academic results at the time of your offer.
Can I receive AUD 40,000?+
The top tier is awarded to the most academically exceptional applicants. The majority of VCIS recipients receive awards in the AUD 5,000–15,000 range. The exact amount is stated in your offer letter.
Is this renewable?+
The VCIS is typically a one-year award. Renewal may be possible for multi-year programs depending on the specific conditions in your offer letter.
Can I combine VCIS with other scholarships?+
VCIS cannot be combined with Australia Awards or other full tuition scholarships. Check your offer letter for specific conditions.
Is VCIS available for all courses?+
Most undergraduate and postgraduate coursework degrees are eligible. Some programs may be excluded — confirm with the University of Sydney Scholarships Office.
