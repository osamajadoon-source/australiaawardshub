RMIT Future Leaders Scholarship

The RMIT Future Leaders Scholarship is specifically designed for students from South Asia — India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Nepal, and Bhutan — offering a 20% reduction in international tuition fees at RMIT University in Melbourne.

Overview

RMIT University is one of Australia's largest and most globally connected universities, with a strong focus on applied education, industry partnerships, and technology. RMIT is particularly well regarded for design, architecture, engineering, IT, and business. The Future Leaders Scholarship reflects RMIT's commitment to South Asia as a priority region.

What does the scholarship cover?

  • 20% reduction in international tuition fees
  • Available for both undergraduate and postgraduate coursework programs
  • Specifically targeted at South Asian students — reducing competition from other regions
  • Applied for the full duration of your program at RMIT

Eligibility requirements

You must be a citizen of India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Nepal, or Bhutan. You must be applying for or commencing an eligible undergraduate or postgraduate coursework program at RMIT University's Melbourne campuses. Academic merit is the primary selection criterion. Check the RMIT website for any additional eligibility conditions for the current round.

Important — check official requirements

Scholarship conditions, values, and deadlines change annually. Always verify the current requirements on the official provider website before applying. Use the "Apply officially" button above to access the authoritative source.

How to apply

Apply for admission to your chosen RMIT program and simultaneously apply for the Future Leaders Scholarship through the RMIT scholarship portal. Submit your academic transcripts, English language test results, and a personal statement. RMIT typically requires a separate scholarship application (unlike some fully automatic scholarships at other universities).

Frequently asked questions

RMIT has a long-standing focus on South Asia as a priority region for international student recruitment and partnerships. The Future Leaders Scholarship is part of RMIT's strategy to attract talented students from India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Nepal, and Bhutan — countries where RMIT has strong name recognition and alumni networks.

RMIT ranks in the global top 250 (QS 2025) and is particularly strong in design, architecture, engineering, IT, and applied sciences. It has excellent industry connections in Melbourne and a very high graduate employment rate. RMIT is known for its practical, industry-focused approach to education.

RMIT's main City Campus is located in the heart of Melbourne's CBD, making it one of the most centrally located universities in Australia. It is walking distance from public transport and the major business districts.

If you are eligible for Australia Awards (Pakistan and Bangladesh are participating countries), that program provides full funding and would cover tuition separately. The Future Leaders Scholarship would be most relevant if you are not eligible for or not selected for Australia Awards.

Engineering (Civil, Electrical, Mechanical), IT, Computer Science, Cybersecurity, Business Analytics, MBA, and Architecture are particularly popular choices for Pakistani students at RMIT. RMIT's engineering programs are ABET and Engineers Australia accredited.
