Australian Visa Guide 2026/2027

Complete guide to every major Australian visa — student, tourist, skilled migration, and temporary resident pathways. Eligibility, costs, processing times, and key requirements in one place.

Student Visa — Subclass 500

The Subclass 500 student visa is required for all international students studying in Australia for more than 3 months. It covers full-time enrolment in any registered course from primary school through to doctoral level, and grants significant work rights during study.

Key facts

  • Duration: length of your enrolled course + 1 month
  • Work rights: up to 48 hours per fortnight during term; unlimited hours during official university breaks
  • Spouse/partner work rights: unlimited if enrolled in postgraduate research (PhD/Masters by Research)
  • OSHC (health cover) mandatory for the full visa duration
  • GTE (Genuine Temporary Entrant) assessment required

Eligibility requirements

  • Enrolled in a CRICOS-registered institution and course
  • Valid Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE)
  • English proficiency — IELTS 5.5–6.5+ depending on course and institution
  • Financial capacity to cover tuition, living costs, and return airfare
  • Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE) requirement — must demonstrate genuine intent to study temporarily and return home
  • Health requirements — most applicants require a medical examination
  • Character requirements — no serious criminal history

Required documents

  • Valid passport (must cover the full course duration)
  • Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) from your institution
  • OSHC certificate covering visa duration
  • Proof of financial capacity (bank statements, scholarship letter, sponsor letter)
  • English test results (IELTS/PTE/TOEFL)
  • Academic transcripts and certificates
  • GTE statement (written explanation of your genuine temporary entrant status)
  • Health examination results (chest X-ray + medical) from approved panel physician
  • Police clearance (required for applicants 16+ from certain countries)

📋 GTE — most critical part of your application

The Genuine Temporary Entrant assessment is where many student visa applications are refused. You must demonstrate why you want to study in Australia, your ties to your home country, your financial position, and that you plan to return home after study. See our GTE Statement Guide for a full walkthrough.

After completing your qualification, you may be eligible for the Temporary Graduate visa (Subclass 485), which allows you to live and work in Australia. Duration depends on your qualification level and campus location — see the TR Visas tab for full details.

Visitor Visa — Subclass 600

The Subclass 600 Visitor visa allows you to visit Australia for tourism, family visits, or some business activities. It is available in several streams depending on your purpose of visit and country of origin.

Visitor streams

Tourism stream

Tourism & recreation

Most common stream. For holidays, sightseeing, visiting friends and family.

Business visitor stream

Business activities

Attending conferences, negotiations, or short business meetings. No paid work permitted.

Sponsored family stream

Sponsored by family

Sponsored by an eligible Australian relative. Allows longer stays for some applicants.

Approved destination status

Chinese citizens (ADS)

For Chinese citizens travelling in organised tour groups. Applied through approved travel agents.

Key conditions

  • No work permitted — working on a visitor visa is a serious breach and can lead to cancellation and future bans
  • No study over 3 months — short English language courses (under 3 months) are permitted
  • Must have genuine intention to leave Australia before visa expires
  • Must have sufficient funds for the duration of stay
  • Health insurance not mandatory but strongly recommended

Required documents

  • Valid passport with at least 6 months validity beyond intended stay
  • Bank statements showing sufficient funds
  • Proof of ties to home country (employment, property, family)
  • Return flight ticket or evidence of onward travel
  • Travel itinerary or hotel bookings
  • Invitation letter (if visiting family or friends in Australia)
  • Health examination (required for some nationalities)

⚠️ Visitor to student — be careful

Applying to change from a visitor visa to a student visa while in Australia can raise GTE concerns. If you plan to study in Australia, apply for a student visa (Subclass 500) before you arrive wherever possible.

Skilled Independent Visa — Subclass 189

The Subclass 189 is a permanent residence visa for skilled workers who are not sponsored by an employer or nominated by a state/territory government. It is points-based — you submit an Expression of Interest (EOI) via SkillSelect and wait to be invited to apply.

🏆 This is a permanent residence visa

A successful 189 application grants you permanent residency in Australia — you can live and work anywhere in Australia indefinitely, sponsor eligible family members, and apply for citizenship after 4 years.

⚠️ 65 points is not enough in practice

The minimum is 65 points, but most occupations are receiving invitations at 80–95+ points. The actual cutoff changes each invitation round. Check the latest SkillSelect data before submitting your EOI.

How the process works

  • Step 1 — Skills assessment: Get your occupation assessed by the relevant assessing authority (e.g. Engineers Australia, ACS, AHPRA) before submitting an EOI
  • Step 2 — EOI via SkillSelect: Submit your Expression of Interest at immi.homeaffairs.gov.au listing your occupation, points, and preferences
  • Step 3 — Wait for invitation: Invitations are issued periodically. Higher points = earlier invitation
  • Step 4 — Lodge visa application: You have 60 days to lodge your full application once invited
  • Step 5 — Health and character checks: Medical examination + police clearances for all adult applicants

Skilled Nominated Visa — Subclass 190

The Subclass 190 is a permanent residence visa requiring nomination by an Australian state or territory government. The nomination adds 5 points to your score and opens up occupations not available under the 189. In exchange, you must live and work in the nominating state for at least 2 years after receiving the visa.

Key differences from Subclass 189

State nomination — how it works

Each Australian state and territory runs its own nomination program with different occupation lists, requirements, and intake caps. Popular states for international applicants include:

  • Victoria: Strong in engineering, IT, health, and education. Competitive — requires strong points and local connection
  • New South Wales: Large occupation list. Requires genuine commitment to live in NSW
  • Queensland: Regularly opens more occupations. Less competitive than Victoria/NSW
  • South Australia: More accessible — lower points thresholds for many occupations. Popular for engineers and health professionals
  • Western Australia: Strong for mining, resources, and engineering. Significant offshore nominations
  • Tasmania: Lower points threshold, easier nomination — must commit to living in Tasmania

💡 Check state nomination portals directly

Each state opens and closes nominations independently. Some states have periods where they are not accepting new EOIs. Always check the state's official migration website for current status before applying.

Skilled Work Regional Visa — Subclass 491

The Subclass 491 is a 5-year temporary visa for skilled workers willing to live and work in regional Australia. It is the easiest skilled migration pathway to obtain — it offers 15 bonus points (vs 5 for 190) and includes occupations not available on other skilled visas. After 3 years living and working regionally, you can apply for permanent residence via Subclass 191.

🌿 Regional living — what this means in practice

Regional Australia means anywhere outside Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth, and the Gold Coast. This includes cities like Canberra, Adelaide, Hobart, Darwin, Newcastle, Wollongong, and hundreds of regional towns. Many regional areas offer excellent quality of life, lower cost of living, and strong employment demand.

Two nomination streams

State/territory nominated

Nominated by a state

State or territory government nominates you. +15 points. Must commit to living and working in a regional area of that state.

Family sponsored

Sponsored by eligible relative

Sponsored by an eligible family member who is living in a regional area as an Australian citizen, PR, or eligible NZ citizen.

Pathway to permanent residence — Subclass 191

After holding a 491 visa and living/working in a regional area for at least 3 years, you can apply for the Subclass 191 Permanent Residence (Skilled Regional) visa. You must also meet an income threshold (approximately AUD $53,900 per year) for at least 3 years.

Regional bonus for international students

If you studied in a regional area as an international student, you receive 5 additional points toward your skilled migration score — on top of the 491's 15-point bonus. Regional study is one of the most powerful points-boosting strategies for international students planning a migration pathway.

Temporary Resident Visas

Australia offers several temporary resident (TR) visa pathways for skilled workers, graduates, and employer-sponsored applicants. These are distinct from permanent visas — they allow you to live and work in Australia temporarily, with some pathways leading to permanent residence.

Subclass 485 — Temporary Graduate Visa

The most relevant TR visa for international students. Allows you to live and work in Australia after completing an Australian qualification.

Qualification & campusStay duration
Bachelor's degree — city campus2 years
Bachelor's degree — regional campus3 years (+1 regional bonus)

Masters coursework — regional3 years
PhD — city campus4 years
PhD — regional campus5 years
Very remote campus+2 years on any of the above

⚠️ Australia Awards holders — 2-year return condition applies

If you completed your study on an Australia Awards Scholarship, you cannot apply for a Subclass 485 until you have returned to your home country and resided there for at least 2 continuous years after completing your scholarship.

Subclass 482 — Temporary Skills Shortage (TSS)

Employer-sponsored visa for overseas workers filling positions Australian employers cannot fill locally.

  • Requires sponsorship from an approved Australian employer
  • Two streams: Short-Term (up to 2 years, can be extended once) and Medium-Term (up to 4 years)
  • Medium-term stream can lead to employer nomination for permanent residence (Subclass 186)
  • Must be on the relevant occupation list (STSOL or MLTSSL)
  • Employer pays the Skilling Australians Fund levy

Subclass 186 — Employer Nomination Scheme (ENS)

Permanent residence visa for workers nominated by an approved Australian employer.

  • Direct Entry stream: employer nominates you from overseas or on a temporary visa
  • Temporary Residence Transition stream: for 482 holders who have worked for sponsor for 3 years
  • Labour Agreement stream: where employer has a formal labour agreement with the government
  • Skills assessment and English requirements apply for most streams

Subclass 407 — Training Visa

For occupational training to improve skills in a field related to your current employment. Not for general study.

Subclass 408 — Temporary Activity Visa

Covers a range of temporary activities including religious work, cultural activities, research, and pandemic event activities.

⚠️ TR visa disclaimer

Temporary resident and employer-sponsored visa applications are complex and highly specific to individual circumstances. This guide provides general overview information only. For any TR or employer-sponsored visa, we strongly recommend consulting a registered migration agent (MARN). Do not rely on this guide for visa application decisions.

Frequently asked questions

The Subclass 500 student visa costs AUD $710 for the primary applicant. Additional family members (18+) cost AUD $530 each; under-18 dependants cost AUD $180. Fees are non-refundable once the application is lodged.

The minimum is 65 points to submit an EOI. In practice, most invitations for Subclass 189 are issued at 80–95+ points depending on the occupation. The 190 adds 5 bonus points (nomination) and the 491 adds 15 bonus points, making both more accessible than the 189 alone.

Subclass 190 is permanent residence requiring state nomination (+5 points) — you must live in the nominating state for 2 years. Subclass 491 is a 5-year temporary visa (+15 points) requiring you to live in a regional area for the full duration, with a PR pathway via Subclass 191 after 3 years. The 491 is easier to obtain but requires a longer regional commitment.

No — the Subclass 600 visitor visa does not permit any work in Australia. Working on a tourist visa is a serious breach of visa conditions and can result in visa cancellation and future bans from Australian visas.

Average processing time for a Subclass 500 is 4–6 weeks, but this varies significantly by nationality, time of year, and how complete your application is. Some applications are processed in days; others take months. Apply as early as possible — at least 2–3 months before your course start date.

Duration ranges from 2 years (bachelor's, city) to 5 years (PhD, regional). Australia Awards scholarship holders cannot apply until their 2-year home return condition is satisfied.
