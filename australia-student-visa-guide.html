Australian Student Visa (Subclass 500): Complete Guide 2026/2027

📅 Updated April 2026 · ⏱ 10 min read · ✈️ Visa & immigration
🛂

The Student visa (subclass 500) is the gateway for all international students coming to study in Australia. Whether you're attending a university, TAFE, English language school, or secondary school — this is the visa you need. Here's everything you need to know for 2026/2027.

Quick facts

Visa: Subclass 500 Student Visa  |  Cost: AUD 1,600 (approx.)  |  Processing: 4–8 weeks (varies by country)  |  Work rights: 48 hours/fortnight during term, unlimited during breaks

What is the Subclass 500 Student Visa?

The Subclass 500 is Australia's primary student visa, issued by the Department of Home Affairs. It allows you to study full-time at a registered Australian institution (a CRICOS-registered provider), live in Australia for the duration of your course, and bring eligible family members with you.

Unlike many other countries, Australia runs a single unified student visa system — there's no separate research visa, postgraduate visa, or English language visa. Whether you're doing a six-week English course or a five-year PhD, it's all Subclass 500.

Who needs a Subclass 500 visa?

You need this visa if you are a non-Australian citizen planning to study in Australia for more than three months at a CRICOS-registered institution. Citizens of New Zealand are exempt (they can study under a different arrangement).

If your course is less than three months, you may be able to study on a tourist visa — but most students who are scholarship holders or enrolled in degree programs will need the Subclass 500.

Core eligibility requirements

1. Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE)

Before you can apply for the visa, your Australian institution must issue you a Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE). This is the single most important document in your application — the Department of Home Affairs will not process your visa without it. Your CoE confirms your course, your study dates, and your tuition fee payment status.

2. Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE) requirement

This is the part most students underestimate. The GTE requirement asks: do you genuinely intend to come to Australia temporarily to study, and then return home? The visa officer assesses this by looking at:

  • Your immigration history (have you overstayed visas before?)
  • Your ties to your home country (family, property, job offers)
  • Your study choices (does the course make sense given your background?)
  • Your personal circumstances (age, English level, financial situation)

Students from countries with higher visa refusal rates (some African, South Asian, and Middle Eastern countries) are assessed more strictly. A well-written GTE statement can make the difference between approval and refusal.

3. English language proficiency

You need to demonstrate English proficiency with an accepted test result. Minimum requirements vary by institution, but the Department of Home Affairs generally accepts:

TestMinimum scoreValidity
IELTS Academic5.5 overall (no band below 5.0)3 years
TOEFL iBT46+2 years
PTE Academic42+2 years
Cambridge C1 Advanced162+2 years
OETB in all components2 years

Note: Your university may require a higher English score than the visa minimum. Always check both requirements separately.

4. Financial capacity

You must show you can afford your tuition and living expenses. As of 2026, the Department of Home Affairs requires evidence of at least:

  • AUD 29,710 for living expenses per year (increased significantly in 2024)
  • Full first-year tuition fees (check your CoE)
  • Return airfare
  • School fees for any accompanying children

If you hold a full scholarship (like Australia Awards, RTP, or a university scholarship), your scholarship letter can serve as evidence of financial capacity — which is one of the biggest practical advantages of being a scholarship holder.

5. Health requirements

Most applicants must undergo an Australian immigration medical examination through an approved panel physician. You'll need:

  • A chest X-ray (for applicants 11 years and older)
  • A full medical examination
  • HIV test (for courses involving patient contact, or if staying more than 3 months in some countries)

Health exams must be done at an approved immigration panel physician clinic — results are submitted directly to the Department online and cannot be done by your regular doctor.

6. Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC)

OSHC is mandatory for the full duration of your student visa. It's Australia's health insurance for international students — similar in concept to Medicare for Australians, but separate. OSHC covers visits to the doctor, hospital care, some prescription medications, and some emergency dental.

Cost is approximately AUD 700–900 per year for a single student (more for families). If you have an Australia Awards scholarship, OSHC is covered for you. If not, you'll buy it directly from an approved provider: Medibank, Bupa, Allianz, AHM, or nib.

Required documents checklist

Gather all of these before starting your online application:

  • Valid passport (must be valid for the duration of your stay)
  • Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) from your institution
  • Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE) statement
  • English language test results (IELTS/TOEFL/PTE)
  • Academic transcripts and certificates
  • Evidence of financial capacity (bank statements, scholarship letter)
  • Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) policy
  • Immigration health examination results
  • National police clearance certificate
  • Passport-sized photographs
  • Birth certificate (sometimes required)
  • Evidence of ties to home country (property deed, employment letter, family documents)

How to apply — step by step

  1. Create an ImmiAccount at immi.homeaffairs.gov.au. This is the Australian Government's official immigration portal — it's the only place to apply.
  2. Start a new application → select "Student" → Subclass 500.
  3. Enter your CoE number. The form will auto-populate some details.
  4. Complete the GTE statement. Be honest, specific, and clear about why you're studying in Australia and your plans after graduation.
  5. Upload all documents. PDFs preferred, under 5MB each.
  6. Pay the visa application charge — approximately AUD 1,600 (subject to change; check the official fee schedule before applying).
  7. Complete health examinations if requested (sometimes requested after lodgement).
  8. Wait for a decision. Processing times vary significantly by country.

Apply early — processing times vary wildly

Processing times in 2025–2026 have ranged from 2 weeks to 6 months depending on your country of passport and the complexity of your case. Countries with historically higher refusal rates take longer. Apply at least 3–4 months before your course starts to be safe. You can check current median processing times on the official Home Affairs website.

Work rights on a Subclass 500

One of the most common questions from international students. Here's the definitive answer for 2026:

PeriodWork hours allowed
During term time48 hours per fortnight (two-week period)
During scheduled course breaks (holidays)Unlimited hours
Before course starts (arrived early)No work permitted
After course ends (waiting for results)No work permitted unless you apply for another visa

The 48-hour-per-fortnight limit was increased from 40 hours in late 2023. Family members who come with you on secondary applicant visas have the same work rights as the primary visa holder.

Common types of on-campus and off-campus work for international students include retail, hospitality, tutoring, research assistance, and aged care — all valid under the student visa.

What happens after you graduate?

This is where Australia genuinely stands out. After completing your degree, you may be eligible for the Temporary Graduate visa (Subclass 485), which allows you to live and work in Australia for 2–6 years post-study (depending on your qualification and location of study).

  • Bachelor's, Master's or Doctoral degree: 2–4 years (more in regional areas)
  • STEM fields: additional 2-year extension available from 2024
  • Regional study (outside major cities): extra 1–2 years added

This pathway makes Australian education one of the most financially rational choices for international students — the post-study work rights and potential pathway to permanent residency (through skilled migration) can more than offset the cost of tuition.

Important — Australia Awards condition

If you hold an Australia Awards Scholarship, you are required to return to your home country for at least two years after completing your scholarship. You cannot apply for a Temporary Graduate visa or any other Australian visa during this period. This is a legally binding condition of the scholarship.

Common reasons for visa refusal

  • Weak GTE statement — vague, generic, or doesn't address the officer's likely concerns about your background
  • Insufficient funds — bank statements that are too recent, show sudden large deposits, or don't cover the full required amount
  • Course doesn't align with background — applying for a course that has no logical connection to your previous study or career
  • Previous visa issues — overstays, refusals, or cancellations in Australia or other countries
  • Health issues — conditions that represent a significant cost to Australia's health system
  • Incomplete documents — missing one required document can result in refusal without a request for more information

Frequently asked questions

Can I study part-time on a student visa?

No. You must study full-time on a Subclass 500. Full-time generally means a minimum course load defined by your institution — typically 4 subjects per semester for a university degree. You can reduce your study load in specific circumstances (illness, academic reasons) with your institution's approval, but your visa requires you to maintain satisfactory attendance and academic progress.

Can my spouse and children come with me?

Yes — eligible family members can come on a secondary applicant visa attached to your Subclass 500. Your spouse/partner can work unlimited hours (no 48-hour restriction) if you are a postgraduate research student. School-age children can attend Australian schools (though tuition fees apply at most schools).

What if my course extends beyond my visa end date?

Apply for a visa extension before your current visa expires. You'll need a new or updated CoE from your institution showing the extended course dates. Don't let your visa expire while still studying — this is a serious breach and can affect future visa applications.

Do I need a student visa for an online-only course?

No. If you're studying entirely online and not physically in Australia, you don't need a student visa. The Subclass 500 is only required if you're physically present in Australia to study.

What is the Student Guardian visa?

If you are under 18, you may need a guardian to accompany you in Australia. That guardian can apply for a Student Guardian visa (Subclass 590), which allows them to stay in Australia for the duration of your studies.
