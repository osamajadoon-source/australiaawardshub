Macquarie University Vice-Chancellor's International Scholarship

Macquarie University offers its Vice-Chancellor's International Scholarship to outstanding international students, providing up to AUD $10,000 to support undergraduate or postgraduate study at its North Ryde campus in Sydney.

Overview

Macquarie University, ranked in the global top 200, is known for its strengths in linguistics, law, business, and the sciences. Located in the northern suburbs of Sydney, its North Ryde campus is a self-contained precinct with strong research and industry links.

The Vice-Chancellor's International Scholarship is a merit-based, partial tuition fee award that recognises academic excellence among commencing international students. The scholarship amount varies up to AUD $10,000 and is applied directly to tuition fees.

What does the scholarship cover?

  • Partial tuition fee scholarship up to AUD $10,000 (amount varies by academic merit)
  • Applied directly as a reduction to tuition fees
  • Available for both undergraduate and postgraduate coursework programs
  • Study at Macquarie University's North Ryde campus, Sydney

Eligibility requirements

To be eligible, you must:

  • Be an international student (not an Australian citizen or permanent resident)
  • Be commencing (not continuing) an eligible undergraduate or postgraduate coursework degree at Macquarie University
  • Demonstrate outstanding academic achievement in previous studies
  • Meet Macquarie University's English language requirements
  • Not already be receiving a full tuition fee scholarship from another source
Important — check official requirements. Scholarship conditions, values, and deadlines change annually. Always verify current eligibility criteria and application deadlines on the Macquarie University scholarships page before applying.

How to apply

The Vice-Chancellor's International Scholarship is applied for through Macquarie University's scholarship portal. The typical process is:

  • Apply for admission to your chosen Macquarie program first
  • Submit a separate scholarship application via the Macquarie University scholarship portal
  • Include academic transcripts and any supporting documents required
  • Applications are assessed on academic merit — a strong GPA is essential

Macquarie University typically has two main scholarship rounds each year, aligned with Semester 1 (February) and Semester 2 (July) intakes. Check the scholarships page for current deadlines.
