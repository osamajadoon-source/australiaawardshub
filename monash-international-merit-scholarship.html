Monash International Merit Scholarship

Overview

The Monash International Merit Scholarship is Monash University's broadest international scholarship — designed to make Monash more financially accessible to talented students worldwide. Unlike most Australian university scholarships, it requires no separate application. You are automatically assessed when you apply for admission, and if you qualify, the scholarship is included in your offer letter.

Monash University is ranked #42 globally (QS 2025) and is a member of Australia's Group of Eight research universities. It has campuses across Melbourne — primarily Clayton (main campus, 25km from CBD), Caulfield (14km from CBD), Parkville, and Peninsula — as well as international campuses in Malaysia and South Africa. For international coursework students, Clayton and Caulfield are the main study locations.

The scholarship is open to all nationalities across undergraduate and postgraduate coursework programs. It is particularly popular among Indian, Chinese, Indonesian, Malaysian, and South Asian students pursuing engineering, IT, business, pharmacy, and education at Monash. At AUD 10,000 per year, it saves AUD 20,000–40,000 over the full duration of a degree — a meaningful reduction on Monash's international tuition fees.

What the scholarship covers

  • AUD 10,000 per year reduction in international tuition fees — applied automatically to your fee invoice
  • No separate application — automatically considered with your admission application
  • All nationalities eligible — open to international students from any country
  • Undergraduate and postgraduate coursework — not available for research degrees (PhD/Masters by Research)
  • Renewable for the full duration of your enrolled program, subject to maintaining satisfactory academic progress
  • Can be held alongside some faculty-specific Monash awards — check individual scholarship conditions

GPA and grades needed to qualify

Monash does not publish a single fixed GPA cutoff for the Merit Scholarship. Based on standard practice across Australia's Group of Eight universities, the typical threshold is:

  • GPA approximately 3.0/4.0 or higher — equivalent to a credit average or above in Australian grading terms
  • Distinction and above (3.5+/4.0 or 75%+ equivalent) — more competitive applicants who are more likely to qualify with confidence
  • International grading scales are converted by Monash's admissions team using equivalency tables — a First Class degree from Pakistan, India, Bangladesh, or Nigeria is well-positioned

Because the scholarship is auto-assessed, there is no separate application to strengthen. The quality of your admission application — including your academic transcripts, English proficiency score, and personal statement — is what determines whether you receive the Merit Scholarship.

Tip — how to maximise your chances of receiving the scholarship

Since the Merit Scholarship is automatically assessed with your admission, the most effective thing you can do is submit a strong admission application: ensure your transcripts are clear and include your grading scale, your IELTS or PTE score meets or exceeds the minimum, and your personal statement clearly articulates your academic background and goals. Apply before the scholarship round closes — not at the last minute.

Working rights and supplementing your scholarship

The AUD 10,000 Merit Scholarship reduces your tuition but does not cover living expenses. Monash coursework students on a Subclass 500 student visa can work to supplement their scholarship:

  • During term: up to 48 hours per fortnight
  • During university breaks: unlimited hours
  • Minimum wage (2026): AUD 24.10/hour — 20 hours per week at minimum wage adds approximately AUD 25,000 per year in gross income

Melbourne's job market for international students is strong in hospitality, retail, IT support, and tutoring. Many Monash students in engineering and IT find industry-related part-time work during their degree through Monash's career development programs. Clayton and Caulfield campuses both have career services that actively support international students in finding work-integrated learning placements.

GTE — what Monash coursework students need to know

All international students applying for an Australian student visa must demonstrate Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE) intent — that you genuinely plan to study temporarily in Australia and intend to return home after completing your qualification. For Monash coursework students, this means your visa application should clearly demonstrate:

  • Why you chose this specific Monash program over equivalent programs in your home country
  • How the qualification will benefit your career when you return home
  • Your ties to your home country — family, employment prospects, property, community
  • A realistic plan for funding your study and living costs in Australia

The Merit Scholarship helps significantly with the financial capacity assessment component of the GTE — it demonstrates that you have received institutional recognition of your academic merit and have reduced your financial burden. Include the scholarship in your visa application financial documents. For a full guide to writing a strong GTE statement, see our GTE Statement Guide.
