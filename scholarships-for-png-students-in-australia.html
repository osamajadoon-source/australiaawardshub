Scholarships in Australia for Papua New Guinea Students 2026/2027

Papua New Guinea is Australia's closest neighbour and one of its most important bilateral partners. PNG citizens receive one of the largest country allocations of Australia Awards scholarships globally — reflecting the deep and long-standing development relationship between the two countries. This guide covers every scholarship pathway available to PNG students pursuing study in Australia.

Australia Awards eligible Largest Pacific allocation Full funding available Research degrees open

Scholarships open to Papua New Guinea students

PNG citizens have access to multiple Australian scholarship pathways — led by the Australia Awards Scholarships, which represent one of the most significant education investments in the bilateral relationship. PNG students are also eligible for Australian university research scholarships, which provide full tuition and stipend packages for postgraduate research degrees.

🤝 PNG — Australia's highest-priority bilateral partner

PNG consistently receives one of the largest national allocations of Australia Awards scholarships globally. The Australian Government's commitment to PNG human capital development is substantial — and for well-prepared PNG applicants with strong DILP plans, the scholarship is genuinely competitive to obtain.

Australia Awards Scholarships 2027

Full funding Masters · PhD PNG citizens High allocation country

The flagship scholarship for PNG applicants, managed by the Australian High Commission in Port Moresby. Covers full tuition at any participating Australian university, return economy class airfare (Port Moresby to Australia), a Contribution to Living Expenses (CLE) of AUD 29,710+ per year, Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC), and an Introductory Academic Program (IAP) orientation in Australia.

  • Annual application deadline: 30 April via the OASIS portal (australiaawards.gov.au)
  • Applications are managed locally by the Australian High Commission Port Moresby
  • Two-year home return condition applies — PNG scholars must return to PNG for at least two continuous years after completing their scholarship before applying for any other Australian visa
  • The DILP (Development Impact and Linkages Plan) is the most important document in the application — it is formally scored by DFAT assessors
  • PNG has specific English proficiency considerations — contact the High Commission for guidance on exemption eligibility

Research Training Program (RTP)

Full funding PhD · Masters by Research All nationalities

PNG citizens are eligible for the RTP scholarship at any Australian university. The RTP provides full tuition fee offset plus a living stipend (approximately AUD 32,192/year, indexed annually). Applications are made directly to individual universities — each university has its own process and deadlines. A confirmed supervisor is required before applying.

Melbourne Graduate Research Scholarship (MGRS)

Full funding PhD · Masters by Research All nationalities

Full tuition + AUD 39,500/year living stipend at Australia's top-ranked university. Open to PNG citizens. Requires a First Class or equivalent degree. Supervisor confirmation at the University of Melbourne required before application. The University of Melbourne has particular strength in public health, education, law, and agriculture — fields closely aligned with PNG's development priorities.

Monash Graduate Scholarship + MITS

Full funding PhD · Masters by Research All nationalities

Combined package providing full tuition (MITS) plus AUD 37,000/year stipend (MGS). Monash is strong in public health, mining engineering, education, and pharmacy — all relevant to PNG's development context. Rolling intake available. Supervisor must be secured before application.

Destination Australia — Regional Campuses

AUD 15,000/year All study levels All nationalities

PNG students willing to study at regional Australian campuses can access AUD 15,000/year through Destination Australia, in addition to any other scholarship funding. Regional study also provides an extra 1–2 years on the post-study work visa (Subclass 485) — a meaningful advantage for PNG students planning to gain Australian work experience before returning home.

PNG's priority sectors for Australia Awards 2026

DFAT determines priority sectors for PNG annually, reflecting the bilateral development partnership. Applicants whose proposed study directly aligns with PNG's priority areas are significantly more competitive at shortlisting. Current priority sectors include:

  • Governance and public administration: Public sector management, financial accountability, anti-corruption, public law, intergovernmental relations
  • Health systems and public health: Epidemiology, health policy and management, community health, maternal and child health, tropical medicine
  • Education quality and access: Education policy, teacher training, curriculum development, vocational education
  • Agriculture and food security: Agricultural science, agronomy, food systems, rural development, agroforestry
  • Infrastructure and engineering: Civil engineering, water and sanitation, transport infrastructure, energy systems
  • Economic development: Economics, development finance, trade policy, natural resource management

Download PNG's current DFAT country profile at dfat.gov.au before applying. Priority areas are updated annually and must be checked for the current cycle.

English language requirements for PNG applicants

The standard Australia Awards English requirement is IELTS Academic 6.5 overall with no band below 6.0. However, PNG has specific English proficiency considerations:

  • English is an official language in PNG alongside Tok Pisin and Hiri Motu. Students who completed their secondary and tertiary education in English may be eligible for an English language exemption
  • Contact the Australian High Commission Port Moresby early to clarify whether your educational background qualifies for the exemption
  • If an IELTS score is required, the British Council and IDP both operate in Port Moresby. Book early — test slots fill quickly ahead of the April deadline
  • If your English score is below the required standard, Australia Awards may fund Pre-Course English (PCE) training before your degree begins

What PNG applicants need to prepare

  • Academic transcripts from all universities attended — must be official documents from the institution. Graduates of the University of Papua New Guinea (UPNG), Divine Word University (DWU), or the Papua New Guinea University of Technology (Unitech) should obtain certified copies from their institution's records office
  • Employment verification letter — confirming your role, responsibilities, and duration of employment (minimum 2 years post-bachelor's for Australia Awards)
  • DILP — Development Impact and Linkages Plan (Parts 1 and 2) — begin drafting in February, allow 3–4 weeks for drafting and revision
  • Two referee reports — using DFAT referee templates from the OASIS portal. Give referees at least 4 weeks notice
  • Police clearance — from the Royal Papua New Guinea Constabulary (RPNGC). Allow 2–4 weeks, as processing times can vary
  • IELTS score — if not eligible for English exemption
  • Health examination — required for Australian student visa; completed at an approved panel physician after receiving your offer

Australia Awards 2027 — application timeline for PNG students

January — Attend Australian High Commission information sessions

The Australian High Commission Port Moresby typically holds pre-application information sessions in January and February. These are invaluable — attend if possible. Check the AHC Port Moresby website for dates.

January–February — Confirm English requirement and book IELTS if needed

Contact the AHC to confirm whether you qualify for an English exemption. If an IELTS score is needed, book for January or February to allow time for a resit before the April deadline.

February — Register on OASIS and start your DILP

Register at australiaawards.gov.au as soon as the portal opens. Begin your DILP immediately. This is the document that wins or loses applications — allow at least 3–4 weeks for drafting and revision.

March — Collect documents and brief referees

Obtain certified transcripts, employment letter, and police clearance. Contact your two referees and give them at least 4 weeks to complete their reports using DFAT referee templates.

April — Final review and early submission

Submit your complete application at least one week before 30 April 14:00 AEST. The OASIS portal experiences technical issues in the final days — early submission is strongly advised.

June–August — Shortlisting and interviews

Shortlisted candidates are contacted by the Australian High Commission Port Moresby for in-country interviews. These are typically held at the AHC.

October–December — Outcomes and offer

Final outcomes announced. Successful scholars receive a formal offer and begin coordinating pre-departure briefings and any English preparation with the AHC and the scholarship manager at their Australian university.

Support structures for PNG students in Australia

PNG students studying in Australia have access to a range of formal and informal support structures that make the transition significantly easier than many other international student cohorts:

Introductory Academic Program (IAP)

All Australia Awards scholars attend a 4–6 week IAP in Australia before their degree begins. This covers academic skills, cultural adjustment, navigating Australian services, and professional development — specifically designed for scholars from the Pacific and Indo-Pacific.

Scholarship manager at your university

Every Australia Awards scholar is assigned a scholarship manager at their Australian university. This person provides ongoing academic and pastoral support throughout your degree and is your primary point of contact for scholarship-related matters.

PNG student associations

Most major Australian universities have active PNG student associations. Melbourne, Sydney, ANU, and Monash all have established PNG communities that provide social support, cultural events, and peer networks for new arrivals.

Australia Awards alumni network

PNG has one of the most active Australia Awards alumni networks in the Pacific. The network provides re-entry grants, professional development events, and career support for returning scholars — helping translate your Australian qualification into impact in PNG.

How to write a strong DILP as a PNG applicant

The DILP is where most PNG applications succeed or fail. DFAT assessors are specifically looking for:

  • Specific development context: Describe a real, documented challenge in PNG that your proposed study addresses. Reference specific policies, sector reports, or national development plans (like the PNG Vision 2050 or MTDP)
  • Named return employer or role: You must have a realistic and credible plan to return to a specific organisation in PNG after your scholarship. Government departments, state-owned enterprises, universities, and NGOs all qualify. Generic plans ("I will work in the public sector") score poorly
  • Measurable impact: Assessors look for outcomes that can be measured — not "improved health outcomes" but "train 20 health workers in Madang Province using a curriculum I will design during my degree"
  • Timeline: Your Development Leadership Plan (Part 2) should include a realistic 3–5 year timeline showing how you will implement your plan after returning to PNG

⚠️ Two-year home return condition

PNG scholars must return to PNG and reside there for at least two continuous years after completing their Australia Awards scholarship before applying for any other Australian visa — including skilled migration, temporary graduate, or employer-sponsored visas. This is a legally binding condition attached to your student visa. Violation results in a debt equal to the full cost of your scholarship.
