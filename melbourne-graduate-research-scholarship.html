Melbourne Graduate Research Scholarships (MGRS)

The Melbourne Graduate Research Scholarships are the University of Melbourne's flagship research scholarship package, combining an international fee offset with a generous living stipend. They are available to high-achieving international students commencing a Masters by Research or PhD at one of Australia's top-ranked universities.

Value

Level

Overview

The University of Melbourne consistently ranks among the top 30 universities globally and is Australia's highest-ranked institution in several subject areas. The MGRS package provides one of the most financially competitive research scholarship packages in Australia, with a stipend that keeps pace with Melbourne's cost of living through annual indexation.

What does the scholarship cover?

  • Full international tuition fee waiver for the duration of the program
  • Annual living stipend of AUD 39,500 (2025 rate, indexed annually)
  • Relocation grant (for students relocating from outside Victoria)
  • Thesis allowance (toward thesis-related costs)
  • Access to University of Melbourne research facilities and support

Eligibility requirements

Open to all nationalities. You must be commencing (not continuing) a Masters by Research or PhD at the University of Melbourne. You must hold a first-class Honours degree or equivalent (typically 75%+ in your most recent completed degree). You must have an unconditional offer of admission to a research degree. The scholarship is awarded on academic merit — GPA, publications, research experience, and the quality of your research proposal all count.

Important — check official requirements

Scholarship conditions, values, and deadlines change annually. Always verify the current requirements on the official provider website before applying. Use the "Apply officially" button above to access the authoritative source.

How to apply

Apply through the University of Melbourne Graduate Research online application system. You must simultaneously apply for admission and the scholarship — they are assessed together. Include a strong research proposal, academic transcripts, referee reports from academics who know your research work, and a CV highlighting research outputs (publications, conference presentations, awards). The two main rounds are October (for Semester 1 start) and March (for Semester 2 start).
