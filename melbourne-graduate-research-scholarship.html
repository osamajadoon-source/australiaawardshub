Melbourne Graduate Research Scholarships (MGRS)

The University of Melbourne's flagship research scholarship — full international tuition waiver plus AUD 39,500 per year living stipend. Australia's highest-ranked university, combining the strongest research environment in the country with one of its most competitive stipend rates for international PhD and Masters by Research students.

Overview

The Melbourne Graduate Research Scholarship (MGRS) is the University of Melbourne's own research scholarship package for international students. It combines a full international tuition waiver with an annual living stipend — equivalent in structure to the federal government's Research Training Program (RTP) scholarship. Both are often awarded together; being awarded MGRS means you are receiving at least equivalent support to RTP.

The University of Melbourne is ranked #13 globally (QS 2026) and #1 in Australia — the country's highest-ranked institution across all three major global ranking systems. It is a member of Australia's Group of Eight research universities and one of only a handful of Australian institutions ranked in the global top 20. For international researchers, this matters both for the quality of your research environment and for the global employer recognition of your degree.

The scholarship is open to all nationalities and is awarded on academic merit and research potential. Two competitive rounds run each year — October (for Semester 1 commencement) and March (for Semester 2). Rolling applications are considered outside these rounds in some faculties. The most competitive applicants combine a First Class academic record with a confirmed supervisor and a high-quality research proposal.

What the scholarship covers

  • Full international tuition fee waiver for the standard duration of your degree
  • Annual living stipend of AUD 39,500+ — indexed annually; was AUD 39,500 in 2025, one of Australia's highest research stipends
  • Duration: up to 2 years for Masters by Research; up to 4 years for PhD (subject to satisfactory annual progress reviews)
  • Relocation grant — for students relocating from outside Victoria
  • Thesis allowance — contribution toward thesis examination and submission costs
  • Access to University of Melbourne research facilities, libraries, computing infrastructure, and graduate research support programs

Eligibility requirements

Open to all international students commencing a PhD or Masters by Research at the University of Melbourne. You must be a commencing (not continuing) student. Key eligibility criteria:

  • Academic achievement: First Class Honours or equivalent — typically 75%+ or GPA 3.5/4.0. A high-distinction research Master's is competitive for PhD applications.
  • English proficiency: IELTS 6.5 overall with no band below 6.0. Some faculties — particularly Medicine and Education — require 7.0 overall. Confirm with your target faculty.
  • Research proposal: A 3,000–5,000 word proposal covering your research question, literature context, methodology, expected contribution, and timeline. Developed in consultation with your proposed supervisor.
  • Supervisor support: A confirmed or responsive supervisor is effectively required in most faculties. Applications without supervisor engagement rarely succeed.
  • Research outputs: Publications, conference papers, thesis chapters, or industry research reports strengthen your application significantly, though not formally required.

Always verify on the official site before applying

Scholarship values, eligibility conditions, and deadlines change annually. Verify current requirements at scholarships.unimelb.edu.au before submitting your application.

How to find and contact a supervisor

Finding a willing supervisor before applying is the single most important action for international MGRS applicants. Without demonstrated supervisor support, even a strong academic record will not succeed in competitive rounds. Here is the most effective approach:

  • 1

    Search Melbourne research profiles

    Visit research.unimelb.edu.au and search by keyword in your specific area. Look for academics with recent publications (within the last 3 years) — not just named professors. Early-career researchers often have more capacity to take on new students.

  • 2

    Read 2–3 of their recent papers

    Understand their active research direction and open questions. This is what makes a targeted email — not a generic enquiry copy-pasted to 20 academics.

  • 3

    Send a concise, targeted email (under 300 words)

    Introduce yourself in 2 sentences. Reference a specific paper and explain exactly how your proposed research connects to it. Attach your CV and a one-page research outline. Do not attach a full research proposal at this stage.

  • 4

    Follow up once after two weeks

    A polite single follow-up after two weeks is appropriate. If still no response, move to your next target. Do not contact the same person repeatedly.

  • 5

    Target 5–8 supervisors simultaneously

    Response rates vary widely. Targeting multiple academics with individually tailored emails maximises your chance of securing a supportive supervisor before your target deadline. Begin this process 3–6 months before your application deadline.

Parkville campus — location and living costs

The University of Melbourne's main campus is in Parkville — an inner-Melbourne suburb approximately 3km north of the CBD. This makes it the most centrally located research campus of any Group of Eight university. The campus is served directly by multiple tram lines (routes 1, 3, 5, 6, 16, 19, 64, 67, 72) with frequent services into the CBD.

Living costs near the Parkville campus are higher than at outer-suburban universities like Monash Clayton, reflecting the inner-city location. Shared accommodation in Carlton, Fitzroy, Brunswick, and Parkville typically ranges from AUD 900–1,400 per month. On the AUD 39,500 MGRS stipend (approximately AUD 3,290 per month), this is manageable with careful budgeting — particularly for single students. The university also offers residential accommodation through its residential colleges (University House, Ormond, Trinity, and others), which are popular with research students from overseas.

Step-by-step application process

  • 1

    Find and contact a supervisor (3–6 months before deadline)

    Use the approach above. Aim for a written email from a Melbourne academic expressing willingness to supervise — this is sufficient at application stage.

  • 2

    Develop your research proposal in consultation with your supervisor

    3,000–5,000 words covering research question, literature review context, methodology, expected contribution, and timeline. Your supervisor may suggest refinements — this is normal and strengthens your application.

  • 3

    Prepare your supporting documents

    Certified academic transcripts and degree certificates, CV highlighting research outputs, IELTS or PTE score, referee contact details (2–3 academic referees), and any publications or conference papers.

  • 4

    Apply via the Melbourne Graduate Research application portal

    Apply for admission and the scholarship simultaneously — there is one integrated application. Target the October round for Semester 1 (February) commencement or the March round for Semester 2 (July) commencement.

  • 5

    Accept offer and apply for student visa

    Outcomes are typically communicated within 8–12 weeks of the round closing. Once you receive a combined offer of admission and scholarship, apply for your Subclass 500 student visa through the Department of Home Affairs.

Being awarded MGRS means you are receiving at least equivalent support to RTP. In practice, the distinction matters little to the student — you receive the same funding package.

The MGRS is highly competitive — Melbourne is Australia's top-ranked university and attracts strong international applicants. The most decisive factors are: academic record (First Class or equivalent), quality of your research proposal, and supervisor support. A confirmed supervisor relationship is often the single most important differentiating factor between competitive applicants. Strong candidates from IITs, leading Chinese and European universities, and other top-ranked institutions are frequently successful.

Yes — the MGRS places no work restrictions of its own. As an international student on a Subclass 500 student visa, you can work up to 48 hours per fortnight during term and unlimited hours during official university breaks. Your spouse or de facto partner receives unlimited work rights as an accompanying dependent of a postgraduate research student. At Australia's minimum wage of AUD 24.10/hour (2026), even 10 hours per week of part-time work adds approximately AUD 12,500 per year to your effective income.

Research Training Program-equivalent stipends are generally tax-exempt for the standard duration of a PhD (up to four years). However, tax rules can change — confirm your specific situation with the University of Melbourne Graduate Research Office or the Australian Taxation Office (ATO) before relying on this assumption for financial planning.

Deferral policies vary by faculty. Short-term deferral for medical, personal, or exceptional circumstances may be possible — contact the University of Melbourne Graduate Research Office directly to discuss your situation. Indefinite deferral is not standard practice. If your circumstances change significantly between offer and commencement, contact the office as early as possible.

The Faculty of Medicine, Dentistry and Health Sciences is particularly competitive and hosts world-class research facilities including the Peter Doherty Institute. The Melbourne Law School and Melbourne School of Engineering also attract strong international research candidates.
