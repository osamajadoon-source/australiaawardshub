Scholarships in Australia for Indian Students 2026/2027

India sends more international students to Australia than almost any other country — and Australian universities compete aggressively to attract talented Indian applicants. While India is not currently eligible for Australia Awards, Indian students have access to a wide range of university merit scholarships, fully funded research packages, and partial tuition awards worth up to AUD 42,754 per year. This guide covers every major scholarship open to Indian citizens in 2026 and 2027, with eligibility criteria, deadlines, and practical advice for IIT, NIT, and Indian university graduates.

Documents

What Indian applicants need to prepare

Australian university and scholarship applications require a clear set of documents. For research scholarships especially, the quality of your research proposal and supervisor communication can be as important as your transcripts. Start preparing 3–6 months before your target deadline.

✓
Valid Indian passport
Must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your intended travel date. Apply for renewal well in advance — Passport Seva centres can have waiting periods.
✓
IELTS / PTE / TOEFL score
Most universities require IELTS 6.5 overall (no band below 6.0). English-medium degree holders from recognised Indian universities may apply for an exemption — check each university's policy.
✓
Academic transcripts
Official transcripts from your university — both bachelor's and postgraduate if applicable. Some Australian universities require notarised copies; check your target university's requirements.
✓
Degree certificates
Your official degree certificate(s). If your degree uses a percentage or CGPA scale, universities typically use equivalency tables — a first class (75%+ or 8.0+ CGPA) is usually required for research scholarships.
✓
Research proposal (research degrees)
A 3,000–5,000 word research proposal is required for MGRS, USYDIS, Monash MGS, and RTP applications. Developed in consultation with your proposed supervisor.
✓
Supervisor confirmation email
For research degrees, a written expression of support from your proposed supervisor significantly strengthens your application. This is often treated as an informal prerequisite.
✓
Two to three referee reports
At least one academic referee. For research scholarships, both referees should ideally be academics familiar with your research capability. Give referees at least 4 weeks notice.
✓
Statement of purpose / personal statement
A 500–1,000 word statement explaining your academic background, research interests, why you have chosen this program, and your post-study plans.
✓
CV / resume
Highlighting research experience, publications, conference presentations, internships, and academic achievements. Include your CGPA and rank if relevant.
✓
Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) quote
Required for the student visa application once you receive an offer. Compare OSHC providers before committing — prices vary significantly. Use our OSHC comparison tool.

Application tips

How to maximise your chances as an Indian applicant

Practical advice for Indian students applying for Australian scholarships and research programs in 2026 and 2027.

01

India is not eligible for Australia Awards — target research funding

Australia Awards is not currently open to Indian citizens. For full funding, a PhD or Masters by Research scholarship (MGRS, USYDIS, Monash MGS+MITS) is the most realistic pathway. These cover 100% of tuition plus a living stipend. A strong academic record — typically 8.0+ CGPA or First Class — is required.

02

Contact supervisors 6–12 months before applying

For research degrees, emailing a potential Australian supervisor with your CV and a concise research proposal is your most important first step. A supervisor's written support dramatically improves your scholarship chances and is often an informal requirement. Target academics whose published work aligns closely with your research interest.

03

IIT and NIT degrees are highly valued

Degrees from IITs, NITs, IIMs, AIIMS, and other top-ranked Indian institutions are well-regarded by Australian universities and scholarship panels. A First Class degree from a recognised Indian institution is a strong foundation. If your university is less well-known internationally, a strong research record or publications will compensate.

04

Apply for IELTS exemption if eligible

Many Australian universities grant English proficiency exemptions to graduates of accredited English-medium institutions in India. Check your target university's exemption policy before booking IELTS — it can save you both time and money. Where IELTS is required, aim for 7.0 to be competitive for research scholarships.

05

Melbourne offers the best community and scholarship density

Monash, University of Melbourne, and RMIT are all in Melbourne and collectively offer more scholarships accessible to Indian students than any other Australian city. Melbourne's Indian community — particularly in suburbs like Box Hill, Clayton, and Dandenong — also provides strong social and cultural support for new arrivals.

06

Combine a coursework scholarship with part-time work

Indian students on partial tuition scholarships (RMIT Future Leaders, Monash Merit, UQ Excellence) can supplement their income through part-time work — up to 48 hours per fortnight during term. IT, tutoring, and customer service are the most common sectors. Australian minimum wage is AUD 24.10/hour (2026), making part-time work a meaningful income supplement.
