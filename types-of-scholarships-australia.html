Scholarship types · Australia 2026

Types of Scholarships in Australia 2026 — Which One Is Right for You?

Government scholarships, university research awards, coursework merit scholarships, and regional programs — the four main scholarship types in Australia explained with real examples, funding levels and eligibility.

The 4 main types of scholarships in Australia

International students often ask “what are the 4 types of scholarships?” — the standard classification applies in Australia too, but each type has specific programs, eligibility rules and funding levels unique to the Australian context.

Full funding 🏛

1. Government scholarships

Funded by the Australian Government or bilateral development programs. Australia Awards (DFAT) and the Research Training Program (Dept. of Education) are the flagship examples. These are the most generous — covering tuition, living stipend, airfare and health cover. Highly competitive. Target citizens of developing partner countries (Australia Awards) or all nationalities pursuing research degrees (RTP).

Full funding 🔬

2. University research scholarships

Offered by individual Australian universities for PhD and Masters by Research students. Examples: Melbourne MGRS, Sydney USYDIS, Monash MGS+MITS. Provide full tuition plus a living stipend of AUD 37,000–42,754/year. Open to all nationalities. Require a confirmed supervisor and a strong academic record (First Class / 75%+). Not available for coursework degrees.

Partial funding 🏅

3. University merit scholarships (coursework)

Awarded to high-achieving international students enrolling in coursework programs (Masters, Graduate Diplomas). Examples: UNSW Scientia (AUD 10,000/yr), ANU Chancellor’s (25–50% off tuition), Monash International Merit (AUD 10,000/yr). Usually automatic — assessed at admission without a separate application. Cover tuition reduction only — no living stipend.

Varies 🌎

4. Regional and special purpose scholarships

Government programs targeting specific cohorts. Examples: Destination Australia (AUD 15,000/yr for study at regional campuses), faculty-specific awards, and industry-funded scholarships. Smaller pools, narrower eligibility, but often less competitive than mainstream awards. Can be combined with other funding sources.

Government scholarships in Australia

Australia Awards Scholarships

The flagship Australian government scholarship for international postgraduate students. Fully funded — tuition, return airfare, AUD 29,710/year living allowance, and OSHC. Open to citizens of approximately 50 developing partner countries. Requires a minimum 2 years work experience and a compelling Development Impact and Linkages Plan (DILP). Annual deadline: 30 April. Two-year home return condition applies.

Research Training Program (RTP)

Government-funded scholarship distributed through Australian universities to domestic and international research students. Provides full tuition fee offset plus a living stipend indexed annually (currently approximately AUD 32,192/year). Applied for directly through individual universities — each university has its own process and deadline. A confirmed supervisor is required. Open to all nationalities.

→ Full RTP guide

University research scholarships

Australia’s top research universities offer their own fully-funded research scholarships that sit alongside (and sometimes exceed) the government RTP stipend rate:

  • Melbourne Graduate Research Scholarship (MGRS) — Full tuition + AUD 39,500/yr stipend. University of Melbourne. All nationalities.
  • Sydney University Research Scholarship (USYDIS) — Full tuition + AUD 42,754/yr — Australia’s highest research stipend. All nationalities.
  • Monash Graduate Scholarship + MITS — Full tuition + AUD 37,000/yr combined package. All nationalities.

University merit scholarships (coursework)

For students enrolling in coursework Masters programs, automatic partial scholarships are the most accessible funding available:

  • UNSW Scientia Coursework Scholarship — Up to AUD 10,000/yr off tuition. Auto-assessed at admission. All nationalities. Distinction average required.
  • ANU Chancellor’s International Scholarship — 25–50% off tuition. Auto-assessed at admission. All nationalities. #1 ranked Australian university.
  • Monash International Merit Scholarship — AUD 10,000/yr off tuition. Auto-assessed with admission. All nationalities.

Regional scholarships

Destination Australia

AUD 15,000/year for international students studying at regional Australian campuses — cities outside Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane and Perth. Can be combined with university merit scholarships. Also provides additional post-study work visa rights (1–2 years extra on Subclass 485). Strong option for students in agriculture, marine science, engineering and environmental fields where regional Australian universities have national leadership.

Frequently asked questions

In the Australian context, the four main types are: (1) Government scholarships — Australia Awards and RTP, fully funded; (2) University research scholarships — Melbourne MGRS, Sydney USYDIS, Monash MGS, fully funded for PhD/Masters by Research; (3) University merit scholarships for coursework — UNSW Scientia, ANU Chancellor’s, Monash Merit, partial tuition reduction, automatically assessed; and (4) Regional and special purpose scholarships — Destination Australia and faculty-specific awards.

Only government and university research scholarships cover living expenses (stipend). Australia Awards provides AUD 29,710/year (CLE). University research scholarships provide AUD 32,192–42,754/year depending on the university. University merit scholarships for coursework (UNSW Scientia, ANU Chancellor’s) are tuition reductions only — they do not include a living stipend.

Australia Awards is widely regarded as the most prestigious scholarship for international students from eligible developing countries — it is a flagship Australian Government program with strong brand recognition globally. For research students from any country, the University of Sydney USYDIS and the University of Melbourne MGRS are among the most competitive and prestigious university research scholarships in Australia.
