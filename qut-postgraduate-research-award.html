What is the QUT Postgraduate Research Award (QUTPRA)?

The QUT Postgraduate Research Award (QUTPRA) is Queensland University of Technology's primary scholarship for PhD and Masters by Research (MPhil) candidates. From 1 January 2026, the stipend increased to AUD $37,010 per year — a jump of $3,373 from the 2025 rate of $33,637, making it one of the more competitive research stipends among Australian universities.

The QUTPRA covers the full cost of tuition for the scholarship period plus a living stipend paid fortnightly. International scholars also receive Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC). QUT is based in Brisbane, Australia's third-largest city and consistently the most affordable major city for international students.

📈 AUD $37,010/yr from 1 January 2026

The QUTPRA 2026 rate is $37,010 — above the national RTP benchmark of $32,192/year and competitive with the Melbourne MGRS ($39,500) and Monash MGS ($37,000). Combined with Brisbane's lower cost of living versus Sydney and Melbourne, the QUTPRA provides excellent real purchasing power. A scholar in Brisbane earning $37,010/year has significantly more discretionary income than an equivalent scholar earning $39,500 in Sydney.

Eligibility requirements

  • Admission: Must meet QUT's admission criteria for a research degree, including English language requirements
  • Enrolment: Full-time, internal enrolment at QUT. Part-time or external study requires separate approval
  • Current students: Current QUT research students can apply if they have consumed less than 1 year EFTSL (PhD) or 6 months EFTSL (MPhil)
  • Nationality: Open to all nationalities — domestic and international
  • Supervisor: A confirmed QUT supervisor with written support is required before applying
  • English language: Must meet QUT's English requirements by the time of EOI submission

How to apply

1

Find a QUT supervisor

Browse QUT's supervisor database at find.qut.edu.au. Contact 5–10 supervisors whose research aligns with your proposed project. Secure a written letter of support — this is mandatory for your QUTPRA application. Begin 4–6 months before the annual round deadline.

2

Apply for PhD admission at QUT

Submit your research degree application through QUT's online admissions portal. Indicate your interest in the QUTPRA scholarship in the financial details section of your Expression of Interest (EOI). Admission and scholarship applications run simultaneously — no separate form is needed for initial interest.

3

Submit your EOI in the annual scholarship round

QUT runs one main annual scholarship round. Submit your complete EOI — including research proposal, supervisor letter, transcripts, and CV — before the round deadline. Current QUT students enrolled in an HDR program receive invitations to apply when the round opens. Check qut.edu.au for exact 2027 round dates.

4

Faculty assessment and offer

Scholarships are awarded to the most competitive applicants based on each faculty's selection criteria. QUT typically announces outcomes within 8–12 weeks of the round closing. Successful applicants receive a combined admission and scholarship offer.

When adjusted for cost of living, the QUTPRA is one of the best-value research scholarships in Australia. Brisbane living costs run approximately AUD $400–$600/month less than Sydney and $250–$400/month less than Melbourne, meaning a QUTPRA scholar in Brisbane has similar real spending power to an MGRS scholar in Melbourne despite the $2,490/year nominal difference.

QUT's research strengths

QUT is particularly strong in the following areas — if your research aligns with these fields, your QUTPRA application is more competitive:

  • Health and biomedical sciences: Cancer research, epidemiology, medical devices, nursing, public health
  • Information technology and data science: Cybersecurity, AI and machine learning, robotics, computer vision
  • Engineering: Mechanical, civil and electrical engineering, smart infrastructure, 3D printing
  • Business and economics: Management, accounting, finance, entrepreneurship
  • Education: STEM education, learning sciences, teacher training, educational technology
  • Environment and sustainability: Climate science, renewable energy, agricultural sustainability

Tips to maximise your QUTPRA application

01

Contact supervisors early — before the round opens

QUT's annual round is competitive. Supervisors who have already committed to supporting your application submit stronger letters. Contact supervisors 5–6 months before the round deadline, not 4 weeks before.

02

Write a specific, tractable research proposal

A strong QUTPRA research proposal defines a specific, answerable research question, positions it in existing literature, and explains methodology clearly. Generic proposals ("I want to study AI in healthcare") are not competitive. Two-to-three pages, formatted with QUT's research proposal template.

03

Apply to the RTP at other universities simultaneously

The QUTPRA is competitive and not guaranteed. Apply simultaneously to Monash MGS, Melbourne MGRS and UQ research scholarships — the documentation (transcripts, CV, English score) overlaps nearly entirely. Different supervisors, same application window.

04

Meet the English requirement before your EOI

QUT requires English language eligibility at the time of EOI submission — not just by commencement. If your IELTS score is expiring or not yet obtained, prioritise booking your test before the scholarship round opens.
