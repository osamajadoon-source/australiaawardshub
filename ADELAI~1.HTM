Overview

The University of Adelaide is one of Australia's oldest and most prestigious universities, a member of the Group of Eight (Go8) — Australia's elite research-intensive universities. Located in Adelaide, South Australia, it consistently ranks in the global top 100 and is particularly strong in agricultural sciences, wine, medicine, and engineering.

The Global Academic Excellence International Scholarship is among the most competitive merit scholarships Adelaide offers to international students. By covering half of tuition fees, it makes a University of Adelaide education significantly more accessible for high-achieving students from around the world.

What does the scholarship cover?

  • 50% reduction in tuition fees for the full duration of the enrolled program
  • Applied as a direct deduction from your tuition invoice each semester
  • Available for eligible undergraduate and postgraduate coursework programs
  • Study at the University of Adelaide's North Terrace campus in Adelaide CBD

Eligibility requirements

To be eligible for the Adelaide Global Excellence Scholarship, you must:

  • Be an international student (not an Australian citizen or permanent resident)
  • Be commencing (not continuing) an eligible undergraduate or postgraduate coursework degree at the University of Adelaide
  • Have demonstrated academic excellence in previous studies — highly competitive; typically top students in their cohort
  • Meet the University of Adelaide's English language entry requirements
  • Submit the scholarship application within the specified deadline for your intake
Important — check official requirements. Scholarship values, conditions, and deadlines are updated annually. Always verify current requirements directly on the University of Adelaide scholarships website before applying.

How to apply

Applications are managed through the University of Adelaide's scholarships portal:

  • Apply for admission to your chosen program at the University of Adelaide
  • Complete a separate scholarship application via the University of Adelaide scholarships system
  • Submit certified academic transcripts demonstrating your academic record
  • A personal statement explaining your academic goals and achievements may be required
  • Applications are assessed on academic merit — GPA is the primary criterion

The University of Adelaide has two main intake rounds (Semester 1: February, Semester 2: July). Scholarship deadlines precede these intakes — check the Adelaide scholarships page for exact dates.
