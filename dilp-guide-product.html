Background

What is the DILP — and why does it matter so much?

The Development Impact and Linkages Plan is a formal document submitted as part of every Australia Awards Scholarship application. It is not a personal statement or a cover letter — it is a structured plan that DFAT assessors formally score, section by section, against a set of criteria. It has two parts: Part 1 covers what you plan to do and learn during your scholarship, and Part 2 — the Development Leadership Plan — covers what you will do when you return home.

Most scholarship decisions come down to the DILP. A candidate with an average academic record and a compelling, specific DILP will consistently outscore a candidate with excellent grades and a vague, generic plan. The DILP is where the scholarship is won or lost — and it is the document most applicants understand least. This guide exists to fix that.
