The difference it makes

See the difference between a weak DILP and a winning one.

This is the most important thing this guide teaches — what "specific" actually means in the context of the DILP.

❌ What most applicants write — likely rejected
"Pakistan faces significant challenges in water supply and sanitation. After my scholarship, I plan to use my knowledge to improve water and sanitation systems in Pakistan and help address the water crisis facing the country."
✅ What winning applicants write — specific, credible, scored highly
"I currently work as a technical officer in a municipal development authority responsible for infrastructure and public services for a township of approximately 7,200 residents. Two urgent public health problems face this community that I am directly responsible for addressing. The water supply infiltration gallery is contaminated by untreated waste — a design flaw posing serious health risks. After completing my Masters in Environmental Engineering, I will design and implement a water supply upgrade to remove contamination from the source. This project is already listed as a priority in the Annual Development Plan — but cannot proceed due to insufficient technical expertise. My scholarship provides exactly that expertise."

The guide teaches you how to find your own version of this — for any professional field, any country, any career stage.
