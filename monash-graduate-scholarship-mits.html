Overview

The Monash Graduate Scholarship (MGS) combined with the Monash International Tuition Scholarship (MITS) provides a comprehensive fully funded package for international students undertaking research degrees at Monash University. Together they cover full tuition fees and a living stipend, giving international researchers one of the most supportive research environments in Australia.

Quick summary

Value: Full tuition fees + AUD 37,145 living stipend p.a. (tax-free)  |  Level: Master's by Research / PhD  |  Deadline: 31 March annually (main round) + mid-year round

What the scholarship covers

  • Full international tuition fee offset (via MITS)
  • Living stipend of AUD 37,145 per year (2026 rate, tax-free)
  • Relocation allowance (interstate and international)
  • Thesis examination allowance
  • Access to Monash's extensive research infrastructure and graduate research community

Eligibility requirements

  • Apply for admission to a Monash research degree (PhD or Master's by Research)
  • Academic results equivalent to Australian First Class Honours (GPA 3.5+/4.0)
  • Strong research experience or publications (for competitive fields)
  • Confirmed or prospective supervisor at Monash
  • English language proficiency (IELTS 6.5+ overall, no band below 6.0)

How to apply

Applications for the Monash Graduate Scholarship + MITS are submitted through the official Monash University portal. Follow these steps:

  1. Review the eligibility criteria carefully to confirm you qualify before starting your application.
  2. Visit the official scholarship page: https://www.monash.edu/graduate-research/future-students/scholarships
  3. Prepare all required documents (see checklist below) before starting the online application — having everything ready avoids incomplete submissions.
  4. Submit your application before the deadline. For competitive rounds, aim to submit at least 2–3 weeks early.
  5. Follow up as required. Some scholarships notify within weeks; others (like government awards) take 4–6 months.

Need help preparing your application? Contact us for personalised guidance on personal statements, GTE statements, and document preparation.

Application checklist

  • Monash Graduate Research application (online)
  • Academic transcripts from all qualifications
  • Research proposal (1,500–3,000 words)
  • Evidence of supervisor support (email confirmation)
  • CV highlighting research experience and publications
  • English language test results
  • Two academic references
  • Copies of published papers or thesis (if available)

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between MGS and MITS?
The Monash Graduate Scholarship (MGS) provides the living stipend. The Monash International Tuition Scholarship (MITS) provides the tuition fee waiver for international students. They are awarded together as a package — you apply for both simultaneously through the Monash Graduate Research scholarship process.
How do I find a supervisor at Monash?
Browse the Monash Research Explorer (research.monash.edu) to find academics working in your area. Email potential supervisors directly with a brief introduction, your CV, and a short description of your proposed research. A positive response from a supervisor significantly strengthens your application.
Is the stipend enough for Melbourne?
AUD 37,145 per year (approximately AUD 3,095/month tax-free) is sufficient for a single student living modestly in Melbourne. Melbourne is one of Australia's most expensive cities, with rent typically AUD 1,200–1,800/month for a shared apartment. Many scholarship holders supplement with part-time work.
Can I take my Monash scholarship to a different campus?
The MGS/MITS is awarded to study at Monash's Australian campuses (primarily Clayton in Melbourne). Study at Monash's overseas campuses (Malaysia, South Africa) is not covered by this scholarship.
When should I apply?
The main annual round closes 31 March for the following year's intake. A mid-year round (typically closing in August/September) is also available for July intakes. Apply as early as possible — scholarship allocation is competitive and supervisor availability is time-sensitive.
