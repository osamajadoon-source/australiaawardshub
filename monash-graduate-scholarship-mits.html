Monash Graduate Scholarship + MITS for International Students

The Monash International Tuition Scholarship (MITS) waives up to AUD 70,000+ per year in international tuition fees. Combined with the Monash Graduate Scholarship (MGS) living stipend of AUD 37,000 per year, the package delivers a fully funded research candidature worth over AUD 100,000 per year in total value to international PhD students.

What MITS actually covers — in dollar terms

The Monash International Tuition Scholarship (MITS) is specifically for international students. It waives 100% of your international tuition fees for the standard duration of your research degree. This is significant because international tuition fees at Monash are substantially higher than domestic fees — and the tuition waiver is often worth more than the stipend itself.

Here is what Monash's international research tuition fees look like without MITS, per year:

For a four-year PhD in pharmacy or medicine, MITS alone can represent AUD 240,000–280,000 in total tuition savings — on top of the AUD 37,000/year MGS stipend. This makes the combined MGS + MITS package one of the highest-value research scholarship offers available to international students anywhere in Australia.

OSHC — what international students must arrange separately

All international students studying in Australia on a Subclass 500 student visa must have Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the full duration of their visa. The MGS + MITS package does not automatically include OSHC — you must arrange and pay for it separately before your visa can be granted.

OSHC covers basic medical and hospital expenses in Australia. It is not the same as Medicare (which international students cannot access, with limited exceptions). Annual OSHC costs vary by provider and coverage level:

  • Single student coverage: approximately AUD 600–900 per year depending on provider
  • With spouse/partner: approximately AUD 1,400–2,000 per year
  • With family (spouse + children): approximately AUD 2,500–4,000 per year

Monash University has a preferred OSHC provider arrangement, but you are not required to use it. Compare OSHC providers before committing — prices and coverage levels vary. Use our OSHC comparison tool to find the best rate for your situation.

Working rights for MGS + MITS scholarship holders

International students holding the MGS + MITS scholarship can work in Australia — this is one of the practical advantages of Australian research scholarships compared to some other countries' equivalent awards.

  • During academic term: up to 48 hours per fortnight (approximately 24 hours per week)
  • During official university breaks: unlimited hours
  • Your spouse or de facto partner: receives unlimited work rights on their secondary Subclass 500 visa, as a postgraduate research student's accompanying dependent
  • Dependent children: can attend Australian schools; school fees may apply for secondary school in some states

Many Monash research scholars supplement their stipend with casual tutoring, research assistance, or industry consulting work within their permitted hours. At Australia's minimum wage of AUD 24.10/hour (2026), even 10 hours per week of part-time work adds approximately AUD 12,500 per year to your effective income.

MITS renewal — staying on scholarship

Both MGS and MITS are not simply awarded once — they are renewed annually subject to satisfactory academic progress. Here is how the renewal process works:

  • Annual milestone reviews: You must pass annual progress reviews conducted by your supervisor and faculty. These assess whether your research is on track and whether you are meeting the requirements of your degree.
  • Satisfactory progress: As long as you are meeting your milestones, completing your annual review documentation, and maintaining enrolment in your degree, both MGS and MITS continue automatically.
  • Maximum duration: Up to 4 years for a PhD (3.5 years stipend + 6-month writing-up period in some cases), up to 2 years for a Masters by Research. Extensions beyond the standard period require faculty approval and are not guaranteed.
  • Suspension: The scholarship can be suspended for extended leave of absence — for example, medical leave or parental leave — and resumed when you return. Check Monash Graduate Research policies for the current leave provisions.

What happens if you get MGS without MITS?

In rare cases, a candidate may receive an MGS (stipend) offer without a MITS (tuition waiver). This can occur when MITS funding is exhausted in a competitive round. Without MITS, you would need to pay international tuition fees of AUD 40,000–70,000+ per year out of pocket — which, on a AUD 37,000 stipend, is not financially viable. If this happens, contact Monash Graduate Research to understand your options before accepting or declining. In some cases, a MITS offer follows in a subsequent round, or alternative tuition funding may be available through your faculty.

What to prepare for your application

  • Monash Graduate Research application — submitted online via the Monash portal
  • Academic transcripts from all qualifications (bachelor's and any postgraduate degrees)
  • Research proposal — 3,000–5,000 words covering research question, methodology, and expected contribution
  • Evidence of supervisor support — an email from a Monash academic confirming willingness to supervise
  • CV highlighting research experience, publications, and any conference presentations
  • IELTS or PTE Academic score (IELTS 6.5 overall, no band below 6.0 minimum)
  • Two to three academic referee reports — referees who can speak to your research capability
  • Published papers, preprints, or thesis chapters — if available, include these

How to find a supervisor — the most important step

MITS is only awarded to students who are admitted to a Monash research degree — and admission almost always requires a willing supervisor. Finding and securing supervisor support is therefore the most important action before applying. Without it, even a strong academic record will not succeed in competitive rounds.

Use this approach:

  1. Go to research.monash.edu and search for academics in your specific area — not just broadly, but by keyword matching your proposed research topic.
  2. Read 2–3 of their recent papers before emailing. Reference a specific paper in your email to show you have done your homework.
  3. Send a targeted email under 300 words: introduce yourself briefly, explain how your proposed research connects to their current work, and attach your CV plus a one-page research outline.
  4. Email 5–8 potential supervisors simultaneously. Response rates vary — some academics respond within days, others not at all. Cast a wide net.
  5. Follow up once after two weeks if you haven't heard back. If no second response, move to other targets.

Begin this process 3–6 months before your target application deadline. Supervisor availability can change quickly as academics take on new students or go on sabbatical.
