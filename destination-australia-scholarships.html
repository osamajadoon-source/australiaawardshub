Destination Australia Scholarships 2026/2027

The Australian Government's Destination Australia program offers AUD 15,000 per year to support both Australian and international students studying at regional universities — one of the least competitive and most accessible government scholarships available. Regional study also unlocks significant post-study work visa advantages not available to city-based graduates.

Overview

The Destination Australia Scholarship is a flagship Australian Government initiative designed to attract both domestic and international students to regional universities and campuses. The program recognises that regional institutions offer world-class education in a more affordable, community-focused environment — and that regional Australia has genuine skills shortages that university graduates can help address.

More than 1,000 scholarships are available per year, distributed across a broad network of participating institutions in Queensland, New South Wales, Victoria, South Australia, Western Australia, Tasmania, and the Northern Territory. This makes Destination Australia one of the most accessible government-funded scholarship programs in Australia — far less competitive than Australia Awards, RTP, or major university merit scholarships, yet still government-backed and worth AUD 15,000 per year.

For international students in particular, Destination Australia offers a strategic combination of funding support, lower living costs, and — critically — additional post-study work rights through the Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) that city-based graduates do not receive. This makes it one of the most practical entry points into a long-term Australian pathway for students who might not qualify for more competitive scholarships.

🗺️ The post-study work visa advantage

International students who complete a qualification at a regional campus receive an additional 1–2 years on their Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485), on top of the standard entitlement. A bachelor's degree at a regional campus gives 3 years (vs 2 in cities). A PhD at a regional campus gives 5–6 years. This is one of the most significant — and underappreciated — benefits of regional study for international students planning a longer-term Australian career or permanent residency pathway.

What the scholarship covers

  • AUD 15,000 per year toward living and study expenses — paid as a cash contribution, not tuition waiver
  • Open to Certificate IV, Diploma, Bachelor, Graduate Certificate, Graduate Diploma, Masters, and Doctorate qualifications
  • Study must be at a regional campus of an eligible Australian university or TAFE
  • Open to both domestic Australian students and international students on a Subclass 500 student visa
  • Awarded for the duration of your enrolled program, subject to satisfactory academic progress
  • Can be combined with some university merit scholarships — check conditions with your specific provider

Eligibility requirements

To be eligible for a Destination Australia Scholarship you must meet all of the following:

  • Be enrolling or enrolled full-time at a regional campus of a participating institution — not a major metropolitan campus
  • Be studying at Certificate IV level or above in a face-to-face or blended mode (not fully online)
  • Meet your institution's academic entry requirements and English language requirements for your chosen program
  • International students must hold a valid Subclass 500 student visa or be applying for one
  • Not be enrolled in a program delivered entirely offshore or online

Academic requirements vary significantly by institution and program. Some regional universities accept lower entry grades than Go8 institutions, making Destination Australia accessible to students who may not meet the competitive thresholds for MGRS, USYDIS, or ANU scholarships.

Verify on the official site before applying

Participating institutions, scholarship values, and deadlines change each round. Always verify the current list of participating institutions and their individual requirements at education.gov.au/destination-australia before applying.

Post-study work rights — regional vs metropolitan

This is the most strategically significant advantage of Destination Australia that most students overlook at the planning stage. Under Australia's Temporary Graduate visa (subclass 485) rules, international graduates from regional universities receive additional work rights that are not available to students who studied in Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth, or Adelaide.

Metropolitan graduate — standard entitlement
Bachelor's degree: 2 years. Graduate diploma/master's coursework: 2 years. PhD: 4 years. No regional bonus.

All the above, plus 1 additional year if your qualification was completed at a regional campus. Effective total: bachelor's = 3 years, PhD = 5 years.

Study at a campus in a remote or very remote classification receives 2 extra years. Effective total: bachelor's = 4 years, PhD = 6 years. Campuses like CDU Darwin and some CQUniversity sites may qualify.

For international graduates planning a long-term career in Australia or pursuing permanent residency — through employer nomination, state nomination, or points-based pathways — extra years on the 485 visa provide meaningful additional time to build work experience, meet skills assessment requirements, and accrue the points needed for invitation to apply for a permanent visa.

Cost of living in regional Australia

Regional Australia is substantially more affordable than Sydney or Melbourne. For international students combining the AUD 15,000 Destination Australia stipend with part-time work rights (up to 48 hours per fortnight during term), the financial position is often more comfortable than a metropolitan scholarship worth more on paper but with higher living costs.

  • Rent: Shared accommodation in regional towns typically ranges from AUD 600–900 per month — 30–50% lower than equivalent Sydney or Melbourne accommodation
  • Transport: Many regional campuses are smaller and walkable or bike-accessible, reducing transport costs compared to large city commutes
  • Groceries and food: Generally comparable to or slightly cheaper than major cities — major supermarket chains serve most regional towns
  • Social and recreational costs: Generally lower in regional areas than in major cities — outdoor activities, sport, and community events are often free or low-cost

How to apply — step by step

Destination Australia scholarships are administered by individual institutions, not centrally. There is no single national application portal. The process is:

  1. Choose your institution and program: Identify a participating regional university and program that matches your academic background and career goals. Confirm the institution is currently participating in the Destination Australia program for your intended intake year.
  2. Apply for admission: Submit your application for admission through the institution's standard admissions process. For international students, this includes submitting your transcripts, IELTS or PTE score, and any additional documents required by your chosen program.
  3. Apply for the scholarship: Apply for the Destination Australia scholarship through your institution's scholarship portal — this is a separate application from your admission application. Most institutions require a personal statement explaining your reasons for choosing regional study and your academic goals.
  4. Receive and accept offer: If selected, you will receive a scholarship offer alongside or shortly after your admission offer. Accept the scholarship within the stated deadline.
  5. Apply for your student visa: Once you have a Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE), apply for your Subclass 500 student visa through the Department of Home Affairs.
