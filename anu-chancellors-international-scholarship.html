ANU Chancellor's International Scholarship

The ANU Chancellor's International Scholarship is one of Australia's most geographically diverse merit scholarships, with nine separate regional categories ensuring students from a wide range of countries are considered competitively against peers from their own region rather than globally.

Overview

The Australian National University is Australia's national university, located in Canberra. It is ranked #1 in Australia and consistently in the global top 30 (QS World University Rankings). ANU is particularly strong in research, public policy, Asian studies, science, law, and social sciences. The Chancellor's scholarship's regional structure means that a South Asian applicant competes against other South Asian applicants — giving students from large sending countries (India, Pakistan, Bangladesh) a fairer shot than in a purely global merit pool.

What does the scholarship cover?

  • 25% international tuition fee reduction (Tier 1 — for strong applicants in each regional category)
  • 50% international tuition fee reduction (Tier 2 — for top applicants in each regional category)
  • Awarded for the full duration of your program
  • Available across undergraduate and postgraduate coursework programs
  • Regional assessment — competing against peers from your own region

Eligibility requirements

Open to international students from all countries, assessed in nine regional categories: South and Central Asia; Southeast Asia; East Asia; Middle East and Africa; Americas; Europe; Pacific; and other categories. You must be commencing (not continuing) a degree at ANU. Academic merit is the primary selection criterion. The scholarship is automatically considered when you apply for admission — no separate application required.

Important — check official requirements

Scholarship conditions, values, and deadlines change annually. Always verify the current requirements on the official provider website before applying. Use the "Apply officially" button above to access the authoritative source.

How to apply

Apply for admission to your chosen ANU program. The Chancellor's scholarship is automatically considered as part of your admission application. Ensure your application is complete and submitted on time. Your scholarship offer (if successful) will be included in your conditional or unconditional offer of admission.
