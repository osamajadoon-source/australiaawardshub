What the ANU Chancellor's International Scholarship covers

The ANU Chancellor's International Scholarship is a merit-based tuition reduction for international students enrolling in coursework programs at the Australian National University (ANU) in Canberra. ANU is ranked #1 in Australia (QS World University Rankings 2026) and consistently in the global top 30 — making this scholarship particularly valuable both financially and for credential quality.

Like the UNSW Scientia, the Chancellor's Scholarship requires no separate application. Your eligibility is assessed automatically from your admissions file. If you qualify, the scholarship is included in your offer letter — the stronger your academic record, the higher the percentage reduction you receive.

Scholarship tiers — 25% vs 50%

The ANU Chancellor's Scholarship operates at two levels based on academic merit:

50%

Chancellor's Award

For exceptional academic performers — typically equivalent to a First Class Honours degree or Distinction+ average (85%+). The highest level of the award. ANU tuition fees range from AUD 38,000–52,000/year by program, so this can represent AUD 19,000–26,000 per year in savings.

25%

Merit Award

For strong academic performers — typically equivalent to a Distinction average (75–84%). This level of the award is more broadly available. Represents AUD 9,500–13,000 per year in savings depending on your program's fee schedule.

🎓

Up to AUD 52,000

For a 2-year Masters at AUD 52,000/year tuition with the 50% award — the largest possible partial scholarship available at ANU for coursework.

📅

Duration

Full program length

🌏

Location advantage

Canberra, ACT

ANU's Canberra location provides direct access to government, policy, and public service employment networks unmatched by any other Australian university.

🔄

Application process

Automatic

No separate scholarship forms, essays, or referees. Submit your ANU admissions application and the scholarship is assessed simultaneously.

⚠️ This is a tuition reduction only

The ANU Chancellor's Scholarship reduces your tuition fees by 25–50%. It does not cover accommodation, living expenses, airfare, or OSHC. Canberra living costs are approximately AUD 18,000–24,000 per year — lower than Sydney or Melbourne but significant. Budget these separately.

Eligibility requirements

  • Citizenship: International students only — not Australian citizens or permanent residents
  • Study level: Coursework programs — Masters, Graduate Diploma, Graduate Certificate. Not available for PhD or Masters by Research
  • Academic merit: Distinction average (75%+) for the 25% award; First Class / High Distinction (85%+) for the 50% award. Assessed holistically from your academic transcripts
  • Program: Any ANU fee-paying coursework program. Not applicable if you hold Australia Awards or another scholarship that already covers tuition
  • English proficiency: IELTS 6.5+ (or equivalent) as required for your ANU program — assessed as part of admission

How to apply

1

Apply for admission at ANU

Submit your application at study.anu.edu.au. Upload certified academic transcripts, English language results, CV and any program-specific requirements (e.g. statement of purpose for policy programs). Your Chancellor's Scholarship eligibility is assessed from this same file.

2

Receive your conditional or unconditional offer

If your academic record meets the scholarship threshold, your ANU offer letter will specify the scholarship award — either 25% or 50% tuition reduction. Not all offers include the scholarship. If you receive one, the percentage is determined by ANU based on your academic profile.

3

Accept your offer

Accept through the ANU Student Administration portal and pay any required enrolment deposit. The scholarship is applied as a direct discount on your semester fee invoices from your first semester onwards.

4

Apply for your Student Visa (Subclass 500)

Use your ANU offer and Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) to apply via ImmiAccount. Your CoE reflects the scholarship-adjusted tuition fees.

5

Maintain academic progress

The scholarship continues each semester you maintain satisfactory academic progress at ANU. Review ANU's academic progress policy before enrolling — repeated course failures may affect scholarship continuity.

If your field is public policy, international relations, governance, law, or economics — ANU is the #1 choice in Australia and the Chancellor's Scholarship makes it significantly more affordable. ANU's proximity to Parliament House, the Australian Public Service, and major international organisations provides career access that no Sydney or Melbourne university can replicate for policy-focused graduates.

Tips to maximise your ANU Chancellor's Scholarship

01

Submit certified, complete transcripts

ANU assesses your scholarship tier from your academic record. Uncertified, incomplete or poorly translated transcripts can result in a lower award or no award. Have documents certified by a notary or official institution stamp.

02

Apply in the first round

Scholarship pools are partially allocated by intake round. Early applicants — particularly those applying 6+ months before their intended start date — face less competition for the 50% tier.

03

Choose the program where your GPA is most competitive

ANU offers programs across multiple colleges. If you are between two eligible programs, consider the one where your academic background is most directly relevant — this can influence the tier awarded.

04

Apply for multiple universities simultaneously

Apply to ANU, UNSW and Monash at the same time. The documents overlap almost entirely and comparing offers — including scholarship percentages — gives you negotiating options.

05

Request a scholarship reconsideration if your GPA is borderline

If you receive a 25% award and believe your record justifies 50%, contact ANU Student Administration with additional evidence (professional recommendations, published work, relevant experience).

06

Investigate ANU research scholarships if eligible

If your proposed study allows a research pathway, the ANU HDR Fee Remission or the Research Training Program (RTP) provides full tuition — a far superior financial package. See our RTP guide.
