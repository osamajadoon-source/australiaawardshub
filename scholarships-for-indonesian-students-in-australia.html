Scholarships in Australia for Indonesian Students 2026/2027

Indonesia is one of Australia's most important bilateral partners and a priority country for the Australia Awards program. Indonesian students have access to some of the most generous government and university scholarships available to international postgraduates — with a strong alumni network and dedicated in-country support from the Australian Embassy Jakarta.

Australia Awards eligible Full funding available Research degrees open Strong alumni network

Scholarships open to Indonesian students

Indonesian citizens have access to both government-funded and university-funded scholarships in Australia. The most competitive and prestigious option is the Australia Awards Scholarship — which covers full tuition, a generous living allowance, and return airfare. Indonesian students are also eligible for all major Australian university research scholarships, which offer full funding packages of AUD 37,000–42,754 per year.

Australia Awards Scholarships 2027

Full funding Masters · PhD DFAT — Indonesian citizens

The flagship scholarship for Indonesian applicants. Covers full tuition at any participating Australian university, return economy class airfare, a Contribution to Living Expenses (CLE) of AUD 29,710+ per year, Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC), and an Introductory Academic Program (IAP) orientation on arrival in Australia. Indonesia has one of the largest allocations of Australia Awards scholarships globally.

  • Annual application deadline: 30 April via the OASIS portal
  • Two-year home return condition applies — you must return to Indonesia for at least two continuous years after completing your scholarship before applying for any other Australian visa
  • Managed by the Australian Embassy Jakarta and Australia Awards Indonesia (AAI)
  • The Development Impact and Linkages Plan (DILP) is the most critical part of the application

Indonesia's priority sectors for Australia Awards 2026

DFAT determines priority sectors for each partner country annually. Indonesia's priority areas reflect the bilateral development agenda between Australia and Indonesia. Applicants whose proposed study directly aligns with these areas are significantly more competitive at shortlisting. Priority areas for Indonesia currently include:

  • Economic development and trade: Economics, agricultural economics, trade policy, development economics
  • Public policy and governance: Public administration, financial management, anti-corruption, public law
  • Infrastructure and engineering: Civil engineering, water and sanitation (WASH), urban planning, energy systems, transport
  • Agriculture and food security: Agronomy, food systems, agricultural science, rural development
  • Health systems: Public health, epidemiology, health policy, nursing, medicine
  • Education quality: Education policy, curriculum development, teacher training, STEM education
  • Climate change and environment: Environmental science, climate adaptation, marine science, disaster risk reduction

Always download Indonesia's current DFAT country profile from dfat.gov.au before applying — priority areas can change between cycles. Study outside the listed areas is not automatically disqualified, but your DILP must make a compelling case for how your proposed field contributes to Indonesia's national development priorities.

Indonesia's strong track record

Indonesia has produced some of Australia Awards' most distinguished alumni, including senior government ministers, university rectors, and leading economists. The Australia Awards Indonesia (AAI) alumni network is one of the most active in the region and provides career support, networking events, and re-entry grants for returning scholars.

What Indonesian applicants need to prepare

  • IELTS Academic 6.5+ with no band below 6.0 — or equivalent PTE Academic score. Book early through the British Council or IDP in Jakarta, Surabaya, Bandung, or Medan. Test centres fill quickly January–March
  • University transcripts with notarial legalisation — Indonesian transcripts often require legalisation by a notary public and/or the Ministry of Education. Allow 4–6 weeks
  • Employment verification letter — confirming your role, responsibilities, and duration of employment (minimum 2 years post-bachelor's for Australia Awards)
  • DILP — Development Impact and Linkages Plan — both Parts 1 and 2. This is the most important document in your application; begin drafting in February and allow 3–4 weeks
  • Two referee reports — using DFAT referee templates available on the OASIS portal. Give referees at least 4 weeks notice
  • Police clearance (SKCK) — obtain from Kepolisian Negara Republik Indonesia (POLRI). Allow 2–3 weeks
  • Health examination — required for Australian student visa. Must be completed at an approved panel physician. Book after receiving your scholarship offer

Australia Awards 2027 — application timeline for Indonesian students

January — Prepare documents and book IELTS

Download the DFAT Indonesia country profile. Book your IELTS test — January or February sitting gives you time to resit if needed before the April deadline.

February — Register on OASIS and start your DILP

Register at australiaawards.gov.au as soon as the portal opens. Begin your DILP immediately — this is the document that wins or loses applications. Allow 3–4 weeks for drafting and revision.

March — Gather notarised documents and brief referees

Collect and legalise your academic transcripts. Contact your two referees and give them at least 4 weeks to complete their reports using DFAT referee templates. Obtain your SKCK police clearance.

April — Final review and submit early

Submit your complete application at least one week before 30 April 14:00 AEST. The OASIS system experiences heavy traffic in the final days and technical issues are common. Early submission is strongly advised.

June–August — Shortlisting and interviews

Shortlisted candidates are contacted by Australia Awards Indonesia (AAI) and invited to attend in-country interviews. These are typically held in Jakarta.

October–December — Outcomes and offer

Final outcomes announced. Successful scholars begin coordinating with AAI on visa, pre-departure briefing, and any English preparation required.

Best cities in Australia for Indonesian students

Melbourne

Australia's largest Indonesian student community. Strong Fryer Monash, Melbourne, and RMIT presence. Indonesian food readily available in the CBD and Clayton. Cooler climate than Indonesia — bring winter clothing.

Canberra

Home to ANU — particularly strong for public policy, governance, and international relations. Direct flight route to Bali via Garuda. Small but close-knit Indonesian community.

Brisbane

UQ is particularly strong for agriculture, health, and sciences. Warmer climate closer to Indonesia's weather. Growing Indonesian community. Lower cost of living than Sydney or Melbourne.

Perth

Closest Australian city to Indonesia — direct flights from Bali. Strong Indonesian community given historical proximity. UWA and Curtin are strong for mining, engineering, and marine science.

How to strengthen your application as an Indonesian applicant

  • Align your DILP with Indonesia's bilateral priorities. DFAT assesses applications partly on how well the proposed study serves the Australia-Indonesia development partnership. Generic goals ("improve healthcare") score poorly; specific, implementable plans with named organisations and measurable outcomes score well
  • Demonstrate leadership in your current role. Australia Awards prioritises candidates with demonstrated leadership experience, not just academic achievement. Highlight team leadership, policy contributions, or community impact in your application
  • Contact the Australian Embassy Jakarta early. Australia Awards Indonesia (AAI) runs pre-application information sessions and can provide guidance on priority areas and common application mistakes. Check aai.or.id for events
  • Apply for multiple scholarships simultaneously. There is no restriction on applying to Australia Awards and university research scholarships at the same time. The preparation (transcripts, CV, references) largely overlaps
  • Don't wait for your IELTS score before drafting your DILP. Start the DILP in February regardless of your English test status. It takes far longer than most applicants expect

⚠️ Two-year home return condition

If you receive an Australia Awards Scholarship, you must return to Indonesia and reside there for at least two continuous years after completing your scholarship before applying for any other Australian visa — including skilled migration, temporary graduate, or employer-sponsored visas. This condition is monitored by DFAT and violation results in a debt equal to the full cost of your scholarship.
