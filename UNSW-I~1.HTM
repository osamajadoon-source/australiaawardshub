Overview

UNSW Sydney is one of Australia's premier research universities, ranked #19 in the world (QS 2025) and particularly renowned for engineering, law, medicine, and business. Its Kensington campus sits just 7 km from the Sydney CBD, near the eastern suburbs beaches — making it one of the most attractive study locations in Australia.

UNSW offers a comprehensive and diverse range of scholarships specifically designed for international students, covering every level of study. Unlike many universities where a single flagship scholarship dominates, UNSW operates multiple distinct awards — meaning more students have a realistic chance of receiving some form of financial support.

Choosing the right UNSW scholarship

The right scholarship depends entirely on what level and type of degree you are pursuing:

  • Undergraduate or postgraduate coursework: Start with the Scientia Coursework Scholarship — the most prestigious and generous. Also check faculty-specific awards and the Global Merit Scholarship, which are less competitive.
  • Masters by Research or PhD: The Scientia PhD Scholarship is UNSW's flagship research award — extraordinarily competitive but among the best-funded in the world at AUD $50,000/year. For most applicants, RTP + TFS (Tuition Fee Scholarship) is the more realistic target.

General eligibility requirements

Eligibility varies by individual scholarship, but common requirements across UNSW international scholarships include:

  • Being an international student (not an Australian citizen or permanent resident)
  • Commencing (not continuing) study at UNSW in an eligible program
  • Demonstrated academic excellence — GPA requirements vary; the Scientia awards require exceptional achievement
  • Meeting UNSW's English language entry requirements for your program
  • For research degrees: a strong research proposal and identified supervisor are essential
Important — check official requirements. UNSW's scholarship portfolio changes each year. Values, eligibility conditions, and deadlines must be verified on the official UNSW scholarships portal before applying. Some awards are automatically considered with your admission application; others require a separate application.

How to apply

The application process varies by scholarship type:

  • Coursework scholarships (Scientia, Merit, faculty awards): Apply for UNSW admission first. Some awards are automatically assessed with your admission; others require a separate scholarship application through the UNSW portal.
  • Research scholarships (Scientia PhD, RTP, TFS): Apply for research candidature at UNSW, including a research proposal and identified supervisor. Scholarship assessment is typically combined with your candidature application.

Given the competitive nature of UNSW's flagship awards, early application is strongly recommended. Faculty-specific and merit scholarships typically have more places and are a good backup strategy.
