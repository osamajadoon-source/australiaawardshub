Buying tips

5 things to know before you buy OSHC

01

Your cover must match your visa dates

OSHC must start on or before your course commencement date and end on or after your course end date. If your visa is extended, extend your OSHC immediately — a gap in cover is a visa condition breach.

02

You don't have to use your university's provider

Many universities suggest a default OSHC provider during enrolment. You are not obliged to use them. You can buy from any government-approved provider, often at the same or lower price.

03

Buy after you receive your CoE

Your Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) contains the official course start and end dates. Buy OSHC after receiving your CoE so you enter the exact correct dates — and avoid paying for unnecessary extra months.

04

Family cover adds up — compare carefully

If your spouse and children are joining you in Australia, family cover is significantly more expensive than single cover. All four providers offer family policies — compare them carefully as price differences are larger for family cover.

05

You can get a partial refund if you leave early

If you complete your course early or your visa is refused, you may be eligible for a refund on the unused portion of your OSHC. Contact your provider directly — the process is straightforward for most providers.

06

Cheapest isn't always best value

The cheapest provider has fewer agreements with medical practices, meaning longer waits and more out-of-pocket expenses. Consider network size and claims reputation alongside price. Allianz has the widest GP and hospital network in Australia.
