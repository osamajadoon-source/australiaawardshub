Overview

Charles Darwin University (CDU) is Australia's only dual-sector university — offering both vocational education (TAFE) and higher education on the same campuses. Located in Darwin, the capital of Australia's Northern Territory, CDU provides a genuinely unique study environment with strong links to Southeast Asian and Pacific communities.

The Vice-Chancellor's International High Achievers Scholarship is CDU's flagship merit scholarship for international students. With only a limited number awarded each year (typically 25–50 scholarships), it is competitive but distinctly less so than scholarships at larger universities — making CDU a strong option for students who want a supported academic environment with a close-knit international community.

What does the scholarship cover?

  • 25–50% reduction in international tuition fees (amount varies based on academic achievement)
  • Applied directly as a deduction from tuition invoices each semester
  • Available for eligible undergraduate and postgraduate coursework programs
  • Study at CDU campuses in Darwin (Casuarina) or Alice Springs

Eligibility requirements

To be eligible for the CDU Vice-Chancellor's International High Achievers Scholarship, you must:

  • Be an international student (not an Australian citizen or permanent resident)
  • Have a record of strong academic excellence — typically a GPA in the top tier of your previous institution
  • Be seeking to commence (not continue) an eligible undergraduate or postgraduate coursework degree at CDU
  • Meet CDU's English language entry requirements for your program
  • Submit a complete scholarship application by the published deadline
Important — check official requirements. Scholarship availability, values, and conditions are updated each intake. Given the limited number of scholarships offered, check the CDU scholarships page early and apply as soon as applications open.

How to apply

CDU manages scholarship applications through its scholarships portal:

  • Apply for admission to your chosen CDU program first
  • Submit a separate scholarship application via CDU's scholarship system — include academic transcripts and any required statements
  • Given the limited number of scholarships, it is advisable to apply as early as possible in the intake round
  • CDU typically notifies scholarship recipients before the enrolment deadline

CDU has two main semester intakes (February and July), with scholarship rounds aligned to each. Contact CDU's international office directly for current deadlines.
