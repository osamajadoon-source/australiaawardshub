City guide

City-by-city detailed breakdown

🌆
Sydney, NSW
Australia's largest city · Most expensive · Best job market

Sydney is Australia's largest and most expensive city for international students. However, it also offers the largest job market, the widest range of part-time work opportunities, and some of Australia's strongest universities including the University of Sydney and UNSW. The higher cost of living is partially offset by better earning potential.

Sydney student tip

Suburbs like Parramatta, Liverpool, Bankstown, and Hurstville are 30–50% cheaper than inner Sydney and well-connected by train. Students at Western Sydney University can live very affordably compared to those at USYD or UNSW in inner-city campuses.

🎭
Melbourne, VIC
Australia's most popular student city · Cultural hub · Strong research universities

Melbourne consistently ranks as the most popular city among international students and has one of the world's most vibrant multicultural communities. It is cheaper than Sydney but more expensive than Brisbane, Perth, and Adelaide. The University of Melbourne, Monash, RMIT, and La Trobe all have Melbourne campuses.

Melbourne student tip

Melbourne has an excellent tram network that is free within the CBD and affordable beyond it. Students in Footscray, Brunswick, or Coburg can live significantly more cheaply than those in Carlton or Fitzroy while still having great transport links to the university precinct.

☀️
Brisbane, QLD
Fastest-growing student city · Warm climate · Great value for money

Brisbane has emerged as one of Australia's most popular student destinations, with its warm climate, lower cost of living than the southern capitals, and the presence of the University of Queensland, Queensland University of Technology (QUT), and Griffith University. Brisbane hosted the 2032 Olympics, driving significant infrastructure investment.

Brisbane student tip

Brisbane's rental market has tightened significantly since 2022. Apply for accommodation early — ideally 3 months before your arrival. Students at UQ's St Lucia campus have good access to on-campus housing which is well-priced relative to private rental.

🦘
Perth, WA
Affordable · Sunny · Remote but liveable · Great outdoor lifestyle

Perth offers excellent value for international students, with consistently lower rents, food costs, and transport expenses than Sydney or Melbourne. The University of Western Australia, Curtin University, and Murdoch University are all well-regarded. Perth's relative isolation from the eastern capitals is its main drawback, but students who choose Perth consistently report a high quality of life.

Perth student tip

Perth's mining and resources sector creates strong part-time work opportunities in trades, hospitality, and services. Students comfortable with weekend and shift work can earn well above minimum wage. Perth's outdoor lifestyle — beaches, national parks, outdoor sports — is largely free or very low cost.

🍷
Adelaide, SA
Most affordable major city · Friendly · Excellent universities · Great food and wine

Adelaide is consistently Australia's most affordable major city for international students. The University of Adelaide (Group of Eight), Flinders University, and UniSA all have strong programs. Adelaide has a compact, walkable CBD, a thriving arts and food scene, and a warm, Mediterranean climate. For students on tighter budgets — or those on scholarships wanting their money to go further — Adelaide is the standout choice.

Adelaide is small enough that a bicycle often replaces public transport entirely — saving $100–150/month. The Adelaide Central Market (twice weekly) offers fresh produce at prices significantly below supermarkets. Students who cook at home in Adelaide can keep grocery costs well under $250/month.

Student concession cards — always get one

All Australian cities offer discounted public transport for full-time students. In Sydney, apply for an ISIC card. In Melbourne, apply for a Tertiary Student concession myki. In Brisbane, apply for a student go card. These typically reduce transport costs by 30–50% compared to adult fares.

2026 minimum wage — AUD $24.10 per hour

Australia's minimum wage increased to AUD $24.10/hour in 2026 — one of the highest in the world. Many student jobs in hospitality, retail, cleaning, and campus roles pay above minimum wage. Casual loading (25% extra) applies to most casual roles, effectively making the rate AUD $30.13/hour for casual workers.

Visa requirements

The AUD $29,710 government requirement — explained

When applying for your Subclass 500 student visa, you must demonstrate access to at least AUD $29,710 per year for living expenses (2026 figure). This is the government's minimum and must be shown through bank statements, scholarship letters, or sponsor evidence.

Important — this is the minimum, not the actual cost

AUD $29,710/year = AUD $2,476/month. This is below the actual cost of living in Sydney and Melbourne (where students typically spend $2,800–3,400/month). It is more realistic for Perth and Adelaide. If you are applying for a Sydney or Melbourne university, budget above the government minimum when demonstrating financial capacity — showing exactly $29,710 for a Sydney university can raise questions about whether your budget is genuinely sufficient.
