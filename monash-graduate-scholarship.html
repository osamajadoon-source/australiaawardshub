Monash Graduate Scholarship + MITS (Research Package)

The Monash Graduate Scholarship (MGS) combined with the Monash International Tuition Scholarship (MITS) forms a fully funded package for international research students — covering 100% of tuition fees plus an indexed annual living stipend. Together they are equivalent to Australia's federal Research Training Program (RTP) scholarship.

Overview

The Monash Graduate Scholarship (MGS) and Monash International Tuition Scholarship (MITS) are the two components of Monash University's flagship research funding package for international students. They are awarded together — MGS provides your annual living stipend, while MITS waives your international tuition fees entirely. When combined, they deliver a fully funded research candidature equivalent in financial value to the Australian Government's Research Training Program (RTP) scholarship.

Monash University is ranked #42 globally (QS 2025) and is a member of Australia's Group of Eight research universities — the country's leading research institutions. It has more than 86,000 students across campuses in Melbourne, Malaysia, and South Africa. For international researchers, Monash's main Clayton campus is where the majority of postgraduate research activity takes place, in faculties spanning engineering, pharmacy, medicine, education, IT, business, law, and science.

The scholarship is awarded on academic merit and research potential. It is open to all nationalities. Candidates with a First Class academic record, a well-developed research proposal, and a confirmed or supportive supervisor are most competitive. Monash accepts rolling applications throughout the year, with a main competitive round closing in March for February commencements.

What the scholarship covers

  • Full international tuition fee waiver (MITS) — covers 100% of tuition for the standard duration of your degree
  • Annual living stipend of AUD 37,000+ (MGS) — indexed annually to CPI; was AUD 37,145 in 2025
  • Duration: up to 4 years for PhD and up to 2 years for Masters by Research (subject to satisfactory progress reviews)
  • Thesis allowance — a contribution toward thesis examination, printing, and submission costs
  • Relocation allowance — available in some faculties for international relocations; confirm with your faculty at offer stage
  • Access to Monash Graduate Research support services, writing programs, and career development workshops
  • Access to Monash's library collections, research computing infrastructure, and laboratory facilities

Eligibility requirements

The MGS + MITS package is open to all international students commencing a PhD or Masters by Research at Monash University. You do not need to be from a specific country or field — the scholarship is awarded on merit across all disciplines. Key eligibility criteria are:

  • Academic achievement: First Class Honours or equivalent — typically 75%+ or a GPA of 3.5/4.0. A high-distinction research master's degree is also competitive for PhD applications.
  • English proficiency: IELTS 6.5 overall with no band below 6.0, or PTE Academic 58, or TOEFL iBT 79. Some faculties — particularly medicine and education — require higher scores. Confirm with your target school before applying.
  • Research proposal: A well-developed 3,000–5,000 word research proposal aligned with Monash's active research areas. The proposal must clearly state your research question, methodology, expected contribution, and how it relates to your supervisor's work.
  • Supervisor support: A confirmed or at minimum responsive and interested supervisor is effectively required in most faculties. Applications without any supervisor engagement rarely succeed in competitive rounds.
  • Research experience: Publications, thesis work, conference papers, or industry research reports significantly strengthen your application but are not formally required.

How to find and contact a supervisor

Securing a supervisor before applying is the single most important action for international applicants. Without demonstrated supervisor support, your application is unlikely to succeed in competitive rounds. Here is a practical five-step approach:

  • 1

    Search Monash research profiles

    Visit research.monash.edu and search by keyword, discipline, or faculty. Filter for active researchers with recent publications in your specific area — not just broad topics.

  • 2

    Read 2–3 of their recent papers

    Understand their current research direction and open questions. This allows you to write a specific, targeted email — not a generic enquiry that will be ignored or deprioritised.

  • 3

    Send a concise, targeted email

    Introduce yourself in 2–3 sentences. Reference a specific paper and explain how your proposed research connects to it. Attach your CV and a one-page research outline. Keep the email under 300 words — academics are busy and long emails are rarely read in full.

  • 4

    Follow up once after two weeks

    A single polite follow-up after two weeks of no response is appropriate. If there is still no reply, move to your next target — do not contact the same person more than twice.

  • 5

    Target 5–8 supervisors in parallel

    Response rates vary widely. Targeting multiple academics simultaneously — with individually tailored emails — maximises your chance of securing a supportive supervisor within a useful timeframe.

Clayton campus — living costs and student life

Monash's main research campus is in Clayton, approximately 25km southeast of Melbourne CBD. Clayton is a well-established student suburb with a large Indian, Chinese, and Southeast Asian community. Shared accommodation near the Clayton campus ranges from AUD 700–1,100 per month — significantly lower than Sydney or inner-city Melbourne campuses. On the MGS stipend of AUD 37,000 per year (approximately AUD 3,083 per month), living costs at Clayton are manageable even for a single person, with reasonable scope for savings.

The campus is accessible by train on the Glen Waverley line (with a connecting shuttle) and by multiple bus routes. Monash residential services offer approximately 4,500 on-campus beds — research students are given preference in some residential colleges, and applications open in October/November each year for the following intake. The campus has a full range of on-site amenities including a health centre, sports facilities, supermarket, and restaurants.

Step-by-step application process

  • 1

    Identify and contact a supervisor (3–6 months before applying)

    Use the five-step approach above. Confirm supervisor support in writing — an email expressing willingness to supervise you is sufficient at application stage.

  • 2

    Develop your research proposal

    Write a 3,000–5,000 word proposal in consultation with your supervisor. Include: research question and rationale, literature context, methodology, expected outcomes, and a realistic timeline.

  • 3

    Prepare supporting documents

    Certified academic transcripts and degree certificates, CV highlighting research output, IELTS or PTE score, referee contact details (2–3 referees), and any publications or research reports.

  • 4

    Apply via Monash Graduate Research portal

    Submit your application for admission and the scholarship simultaneously through the Monash Graduate Research application system — there is no separate scholarship application form for MGS + MITS.

  • 5

    Accept offer and apply for student visa

    Outcomes for the March main round are typically communicated within 8–12 weeks. Rolling applications may take longer. Once you receive a combined offer of admission and scholarship, apply for your Subclass 500 student visa through the Department of Home Affairs.
