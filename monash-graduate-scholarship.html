Monash Graduate Scholarship + MITS (Research Package)

The Monash Graduate Scholarship (MGS) combined with the Monash International Tuition Scholarship (MITS) forms a fully funded package for international research students — covering both living expenses and 100% of tuition fees. Together, they are the equivalent of the federal RTP scholarship package.

Overview

Monash University's research scholarship package combines two components: the Monash Graduate Scholarship (MGS) which provides the living stipend, and the Monash International Tuition Scholarship (MITS) which covers tuition fees. Both are typically awarded together to successful international research applicants. Monash has strong research programs across engineering, pharmacy, medicine, IT, business, and education.

What does the scholarship cover?

  • Full international tuition fee waiver (MITS component)
  • Annual living stipend of AUD 37,145 (MGS component — 2025 rate, indexed annually)
  • Awarded for up to 3.5 years for PhD (subject to satisfactory progress)
  • Access to Monash Graduate Research support services
  • Relocation allowance may be available in some faculties

Eligibility requirements

Open to international students commencing a PhD or Masters by Research at Monash. You must demonstrate high academic achievement — typically First Class Honours or equivalent (75%+). Research experience, publications, and the quality of your research proposal are key factors. You must secure a supervisor before applying in most cases. Apply to the Monash Graduate Research School.

Important — check official requirements

Scholarship conditions, values, and deadlines change annually. Always verify the current requirements on the official provider website before applying. Use the "Apply officially" button above to access the authoritative source.

How to apply

Contact potential supervisors at Monash before applying. Once you have a supervisor's support, apply for admission and the scholarship package through the Monash Graduate Research application system. Include your research proposal, transcripts, CV, and referee reports. Applications are considered in multiple rounds throughout the year, with the main round closing in March.
