Overview

The Australia Awards Scholarships are the Australian Government's flagship international scholarship program, funded by the Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT). They are among the most prestigious and generous scholarships available to students from developing countries, covering all major costs associated with studying in Australia.

Quick summary

Value: Full tuition + return airfare + AUD 29,710 living allowance p.a. + OSHC  |  Level: Bachelor's / Master's / PhD  |  Deadline: 30 April 2027 (annual cycle, 14:00 AEST)

What the scholarship covers

  • Full tuition fees at any participating Australian university or TAFE
  • Return economy class airfare (most direct route)
  • Contribution to Living Expenses (CLE) — AUD 29,710+ per year
  • Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the award holder
  • Establishment allowance (one-off payment for initial costs)
  • Introductory Academic Program (IAP) before study begins
  • Pre-course English (PCE) training if required
  • Academic support throughout the program

Eligibility requirements

  • Citizen of a participating country (Asia, Pacific, Africa, Middle East)
  • Not hold Australian citizenship or permanent residency
  • Not be applying to study in your country of citizenship
  • Meet academic requirements of your chosen institution
  • IELTS 6.5+ (no band below 6.0) or equivalent
  • At least 2 years of relevant work experience (postgraduate)
  • Demonstrate leadership potential and commitment to development
  • No current Australian visa or application in progress

How to apply

Applications for the Australia Awards Scholarships 2027 are submitted through the official Australian Government (DFAT) portal. Follow these steps:

  1. Review the eligibility criteria carefully to confirm you qualify before starting your application.
  2. Visit the official scholarship page: https://www.dfat.gov.au/people-to-people/australia-awards/australia-awards-scholarships
  3. Prepare all required documents (see checklist below) before starting the online application — having everything ready avoids incomplete submissions.
  4. Submit your application before the deadline. For competitive rounds, aim to submit at least 2–3 weeks early.
  5. Follow up as required. Some scholarships notify within weeks; others (like government awards) take 4–6 months.

Need help preparing your application? Contact us for personalised guidance on personal statements, GTE statements, and document preparation.

Application checklist

  • Valid passport (must cover full study period)
  • Proof of citizenship of a participating country
  • Academic transcripts (all qualifications)
  • English test results (IELTS/TOEFL/PTE)
  • Statement of purpose / personal statement
  • Curriculum vitae (CV)
  • Two referee reports (using DFAT templates)
  • Evidence of at least 2 years work experience
  • Country-specific supporting documents (check country profile)

Frequently asked questions

Which countries are eligible for Australia Awards?
Approximately 50 partner countries across the Indo-Pacific, Africa, and the Middle East. Major eligible countries include Pakistan, India, Indonesia, Bangladesh, Nepal, Sri Lanka, Philippines, Vietnam, Ghana, Kenya, Nigeria, Ethiopia, and many Pacific Island nations. Check the DFAT participating countries page for the current complete list.
Can I choose any university in Australia?
You nominate up to three study options and three Australian institutions during the application process. DFAT then places successful applicants based on your preferences and institutional availability. You cannot be guaranteed your first-choice university, but your preferences are taken into account.
Is there a two-year return requirement?
Yes — this is a legally binding condition. You must return to your home country for at least two years after completing your scholarship. You cannot apply for any other Australian visa (including the Temporary Graduate visa) during this two-year period. Failure to comply results in a debt equal to the total cost of your scholarship.
Can I bring my family to Australia?
Yes. Eligible family members (spouse/partner and dependent children) can accompany you as secondary applicants. However, the scholarship does not cover their living costs — you are responsible for demonstrating you can support them financially.
What fields of study are eligible?
Most fields are covered, but each participating country has priority areas aligned with its development needs. Study in aviation, nuclear technology, and military fields is excluded. Check your country's profile on the DFAT website for specific priority areas.
