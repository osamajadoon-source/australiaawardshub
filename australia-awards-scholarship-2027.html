Australia Awards Scholarships 2027

The Australia Awards Scholarships are the Australian Government's flagship international scholarship program, funded by the Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT). They are among the most prestigious and comprehensive scholarships available to international students from developing countries, covering tuition, airfare, living costs, and health insurance for the full duration of study.

Overview

Australia Awards Scholarships (formerly Australian Development Scholarships) have been running for over 70 years and represent Australia's most significant people-to-people investment in developing countries. The program targets citizens from approximately 50 partner countries — primarily in the Indo-Pacific region, Africa, and the Middle East — who demonstrate leadership potential and a commitment to contributing to their home country's development after graduating.

What does the scholarship cover?

  • Full tuition fees paid directly to the university
  • Return economy class airfare (Australia and home country)
  • Establishment allowance (one-off, toward accommodation and study materials)
  • Contribution to Living Expenses (CLE) — paid fortnightly, currently AUD 29,710+ per year
  • Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the duration of the award
  • Introductory Academic Program (IAP) — pre-study orientation in Australia
  • Pre-course English language training (if required)
  • Academic support throughout the program
  • Supplementary academic support if needed

Eligibility requirements

You must be a citizen of a participating country (check the DFAT website for the current list). You must not be an Australian citizen, permanent resident, or hold Australian citizenship by descent. Most programs require a minimum of 2 years of full-time work experience after completing your undergraduate degree. You must demonstrate leadership potential and a commitment to development in your home country. You must meet English language requirements (usually IELTS 6.5 overall, no band below 6.0). Australia Awards are not available for military training, nuclear technology, or aviation.

Important — check official requirements

Scholarship conditions, values, and deadlines change annually. Always verify the current requirements on the official provider website before applying. Use the "Apply officially" button above to access the authoritative source.

How to apply

Applications are submitted through the OASIS (Online Australia Scholarships Information System) portal or, for some countries, by hard copy. Check your country's specific application portal on the DFAT participating countries page. The application typically requires: completed application form, personal statement, referee reports, academic transcripts, English test results, and evidence of work experience. Applications open on 1 February each year and close on 30 April at 14:00 AEST.
