Overview

The Australia Awards Scholarships are the Australian Government's flagship international scholarship program, managed by the Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT). They are among the most generous scholarships available to citizens of developing countries — covering all major costs of studying in Australia, including full tuition, return airfare, and a fortnightly living allowance for the full duration of your degree.

Australia Awards have been running since 1955 and have supported over 100,000 alumni across the Indo-Pacific, Africa, and the Middle East. Scholars are selected not just on academic merit but on leadership potential and the credibility of their plan to apply their Australian qualification to their home country's development challenges. This is what makes Australia Awards distinct — and why the DILP is the most critical document in every application.

What the scholarship covers

  • Full tuition fees at any participating Australian university or TAFE — for the complete duration of your enrolled program
  • Return economy class airfare — most direct route between your home country and Australia, both at commencement and on completion
  • Contribution to Living Expenses (CLE) — AUD 29,710+ per year, paid fortnightly to your Australian bank account
  • Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) for the award holder — you do not pay for health insurance separately
  • Establishment allowance — a one-off payment on arrival to cover initial setup costs
  • Introductory Academic Program (IAP) — a pre-study orientation program covering academic skills, cultural adjustment, and professional development
  • Pre-Course English (PCE) training — funded by DFAT if your English score is below the required standard
  • Academic support and mentoring throughout your program, coordinated by the scholarship manager at your institution

Eligibility requirements

  • Citizen and resident of a participating country — approximately 50 countries across Asia, Pacific, Africa, and the Middle East
  • Not an Australian citizen, permanent resident, or applicant for Australian permanent residency
  • Not currently studying or residing in Australia at the time of application
  • IELTS Academic 6.5 overall with no band below 6.0 — or equivalent PTE Academic / TOEFL iBT — valid as of 1 January of your proposed commencement year
  • At least 2 years of relevant post-bachelor's work experience (for postgraduate applications)
  • Meet the academic entry requirements of your nominated Australian institution
  • Demonstrate leadership potential and a credible, specific plan to use your qualification for development impact at home

Eligibility for specific study fields and levels varies by country. Check the DFAT country profile for your home country for country-specific requirements and any additional criteria.

Priority sectors for 2027

Each participating country has DFAT-determined priority sectors — fields where Australia Awards investment is expected to deliver the most development impact. Applicants whose proposed study aligns directly with their country's priority areas are significantly more competitive at shortlisting. Priority areas vary by country, but these are the most common across the Indo-Pacific and Africa:

HealthPublic health, health systems, epidemiology, nursing, medicine
InfrastructureCivil, water, energy & transport engineering; WASH; urban planning
AgricultureAgronomy, food security, agricultural economics, climate-resilient farming
EducationEducation policy, curriculum, STEM education, teacher training
GovernancePublic administration, law, economics, financial management
Climate & environmentEnvironmental science, disaster risk reduction, climate adaptation

Download your country's DFAT country profile before applying — priority areas can change between cycles. Study outside listed areas is not automatically disqualified, but your DILP must make a compelling case for the development contribution of your proposed field.

The DILP — the most important document in your application

The Development Impact and Linkages Plan (DILP) is formally scored by DFAT assessors. It is not a personal statement or motivation letter — it is a structured development plan with two distinct parts:

  • Part 1 — Study and Development Linkages Plan: What you plan to study, how it connects to your home country's specific development challenges, and how you will apply your learning upon return.
  • Part 2 — Development Leadership Plan: A specific, credible plan for how you will contribute to measurable development outcomes after completing your scholarship. Assessors look for named organisations, specific roles, realistic timelines, and impact that can be measured.

Most Australia Awards applications succeed or fail on the DILP. A candidate with an average academic record and a specific, well-argued DILP will consistently outscore a candidate with excellent grades and a vague plan. The most common rejection reasons: generic goals, no named organisation to return to, and impact that cannot be attributed to the scholarship.

How to apply — step by step

Applications are submitted through the OASIS portal at australiaawards.gov.au. The portal opens in February and closes at 14:00 AEST on 30 April each year. Late submissions are never accepted.

  • 1

    January — Check your country profile and book IELTS

    Download the DFAT country profile for your country. Confirm your field is aligned with priority areas. Book your IELTS — test centres in Lahore, Dhaka, Lagos, Nairobi, and Abuja fill quickly in February and March. Aim for a January or February sitting to allow time for a resit.

  • 2

    February — Register on OASIS and start your DILP

    Register on OASIS as soon as the portal opens. Begin your DILP immediately — allow 3–4 weeks for drafting and revision. This is not a document to write in the final week.

  • 3

    March — Gather documents and brief referees

    Collect transcripts (with attestation or translation where required), employment verification letters, and police clearance. Give your two referees at least 4 weeks to complete their reports using DFAT referee templates.

  • 4

    April — Finalise and submit at least one week early

    Nominate up to three courses and three Australian institutions. Review your complete DILP one final time. Submit at least one week before 30 April — the OASIS system experiences heavy traffic and occasional technical issues in the final days.

  • 5

    June–August — Shortlisting and interviews

    Shortlisted candidates are contacted for in-country interviews at the Australian High Commission or Embassy. Prepare to clearly explain your DILP, your return plan, and how your qualification addresses your country's development challenges.

  • 6

    October–December — Outcomes and visa

    Final outcomes are announced between October and December. Successful scholars receive a formal offer and begin coordinating visa, pre-departure briefings, and any English training with the scholarship manager.

Application checklist

  • Valid passport — must cover the full study period
  • Proof of citizenship of a participating country
  • Academic transcripts for all qualifications — with attestation or translation where required
  • IELTS Academic 6.5+ (no band below 6.0) — or equivalent PTE / TOEFL score
  • Development Impact and Linkages Plan (DILP) — Parts 1 and 2
  • Statement of purpose / personal statement
  • Curriculum vitae (CV) — max 4 pages, highlighting leadership and development-sector experience
  • Two referee reports — completed using DFAT referee templates available on OASIS
  • Employment verification letter — confirming role, responsibilities, and duration (at least 2 years post-bachelor's)
  • Country-specific documents — check your DFAT country profile for additional requirements

The two-year home return condition

⚠️ Legally binding — read carefully

You must return to your home country and reside there for at least two continuous years after completing your scholarship before applying for any other Australian visa. This condition is formally attached to your student visa and monitored by DFAT. Failure to comply results in a debt to the Commonwealth equal to the total scholarship cost — which for a two-year Master's can exceed AUD 150,000.

During the two-year period you cannot apply for any Australian visa, including: the Temporary Graduate visa (485), employer-sponsored visas (482, 186, 494), or skilled migration visas (189, 190, 491). The two-year period begins from your scholarship completion date, not your departure from Australia. This condition does not apply to university research scholarships (MGRS, USYDIS, Monash MGS).
