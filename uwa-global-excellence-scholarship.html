What is the UWA Global Excellence Scholarship?

The UWA Global Excellence Scholarship is the University of Western Australia's flagship merit scholarship for international undergraduate and postgraduate coursework students. It provides up to AUD $12,000 per year in tuition fee reduction for postgraduate students — automatically assessed at admission, with no separate scholarship application required.

UWA is ranked in the global top 100 (QS World University Rankings) and is Perth's — and Western Australia's — highest-ranked university. The scholarship is merit-tiered: the higher your GPA or ATAR equivalent, the larger the percentage reduction you receive.

Important: scholarship is tiered by GPA — apply early for best assessment

The exact value of your Global Excellence Scholarship depends on your GPA or equivalent ATAR/WAM. UWA uses an equivalency conversion for international qualifications. Higher-performing applicants receive a higher annual tuition reduction. The final award level is confirmed only after UWA receives your official academic transcripts — if you apply before your final transcripts are available, UWA assesses you for the International Student Award first and upgrades to Global Excellence when transcripts arrive. Apply early to allow time for this upgrade process before your intake begins.

Eligibility requirements

  • Student type: International students only (not Australian citizens, permanent residents or New Zealand citizens)
  • Study level: Eligible undergraduate and postgraduate coursework programs. Not available for Higher Degree by Research (HDR) students — research students should apply for the UWA RTP or HDR Scholarship instead
  • Academic merit: Assessed against a GPA or ATAR equivalency table published by UWA. A Distinction average (75%+) or equivalent is generally required for postgraduate applicants to qualify for a meaningful scholarship level
  • Commencement: Must be commencing an eligible UWA coursework program. Not available if you are returning to a prior UWA course of study
  • Exclusions: Cannot be held with other UWA tuition scholarships or academic awards. UWA awards the higher-value scholarship if you qualify for multiple awards simultaneously
  • Program: Must be enrolled in an eligible fee-paying UWA coursework program (most Masters programs are eligible — check UWA's scholarship page for program-specific exclusions)

How the scholarship is assessed

1

Apply for admission to UWA

Submit your admissions application at uwa.edu.au. Include all academic transcripts, English language results and any other program-specific documents. Your Global Excellence Scholarship eligibility is assessed from this file — no separate scholarship form is needed.

2

Receive your admission offer

UWA assesses your scholarship eligibility as part of the admissions review. If your predicted or completed academic results meet the scholarship threshold, the scholarship will be included in your admission offer letter, specifying the annual scholarship amount.

3

Final scholarship confirmed on official transcripts

If you applied before receiving your final academic results, UWA confirms the final scholarship level once your official transcripts are submitted. Students who did not initially qualify for Global Excellence may be upgraded from the International Student Award if their final transcripts meet the higher threshold.

4

Accept your offer

Accept your UWA offer through the admissions portal. The scholarship is applied automatically as a deduction from your semester tuition fee invoices. You pay the balance after the discount.

5

Maintain academic standing

The scholarship continues each semester provided you maintain satisfactory academic progress and remain enrolled full-time in your eligible coursework program. Review UWA's scholarship continuation criteria for specific GPA maintenance requirements.

UWA's key advantage is Perth's significantly lower cost of living compared to Sydney, Melbourne and Canberra — making the real value of any given scholarship amount higher.

Why UWA and Perth?

  • Global top 100 ranked university — consistently ranked among Australia's top five universities across most rankings frameworks
  • Perth cost of living: AUD $1,570–$2,200/month total living expenses — significantly less than Sydney ($2,100–$2,850) and Melbourne ($1,880–$2,630). See our cost of living guide
  • Location for Pacific and South-East Asian students: Perth is Australia's closest major city to South-East Asia and Indonesia. Direct flights from Bali, Jakarta and Singapore. Very active Indonesian, Malaysian and Filipino student communities
  • Research strengths: UWA excels in marine science, mining engineering, agriculture, medicine, law and public policy. Strong industry connections to Western Australia's mining and resources sector
  • Forrest Research Foundation: UWA students who progress to a PhD can apply for the Forrest Scholarship — a $270,000+ package that is only available at WA universities. Completing a Masters at UWA positions you perfectly for this next step
