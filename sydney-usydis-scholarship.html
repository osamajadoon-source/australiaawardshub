University of Sydney International Stipend (USYDIS)

The University of Sydney International Stipend (USYDIS) is one of Australia's most generous research scholarships, offering international PhD and Masters by Research students a full tuition fee offset plus one of the highest stipends available at any Australian university — currently AUD 42,754 per year.

Overview

The University of Sydney is Australia's first university, founded in 1850, and consistently ranks in the global top 20–50 across multiple subject areas. The USYDIS is awarded on the basis of academic merit and research potential, and is designed to attract the world's best research students to Sydney. The stipend is notably higher than most comparable Australian scholarships, reflecting Sydney's higher cost of living.

What does the scholarship cover?

  • Full international tuition fee offset (covers all tuition for the program duration)
  • Annual living stipend of AUD 42,754 (2025 rate — one of Australia's highest)
  • Paid for up to 14 research periods for PhD (approximately 3.5 years)
  • Paid for up to 7 research periods for Masters by Research (approximately 2 years)
  • Access to University of Sydney research facilities, libraries and support services

Eligibility requirements

You must be an international student (not an Australian citizen or permanent resident). You must hold an unconditional offer of admission or be currently enrolled in a Masters by Research or PhD at the University of Sydney. You must demonstrate high academic merit — typically a First Class Honours degree or equivalent (WAM of 80+ or equivalent GPA). Research experience, publications, and the quality of your research proposal are significant factors.

Important — check official requirements

Scholarship conditions, values, and deadlines change annually. Always verify the current requirements on the official provider website before applying. Use the "Apply officially" button above to access the authoritative source.

How to apply

Apply for admission to a higher degree by research through the Sydney Courses portal. The USYDIS application is submitted as part of the admissions process — there is no separate scholarship application form. Ensure your admission application is submitted before the scholarship round deadline. You will need: academic transcripts, English language test results (IELTS 6.5+ or equivalent), a research proposal, referee reports, and a CV.
