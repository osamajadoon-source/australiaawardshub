Overview

The University of Sydney International Stipend (USYDIS) is the highest-value research stipend offered at any Australian university — AUD 42,754 per year, combined with a full international tuition waiver. It is the University of Sydney's primary mechanism for attracting outstanding international researchers across all disciplines, and reflects Sydney's position as one of the world's most globally connected research universities.

The University of Sydney was founded in 1850 — Australia's first university — and is ranked #18 globally (QS 2026) and #3 in Australia. It is a member of Australia's Group of Eight research universities and a founding member of the Universitas 21 and Association of Pacific Rim Universities international networks. With more than 8,000 academic and research staff across 16 faculties and schools, it offers an exceptionally broad research environment for international PhD and Masters by Research candidates.

Unlike Melbourne (which uses standard semesters), the University of Sydney operates on a research period system — four research periods per year. This creates two scholarship application rounds each year with specific deadlines. Understanding this timeline is important for planning your application.

What the scholarship covers

  • Full international tuition fee waiver for the standard duration of your enrolled degree
  • Annual living stipend of AUD 42,754 — Australia's highest research scholarship stipend (2025 rate, indexed annually)
  • Duration: up to 14 research periods for PhD (approximately 3.5 years); up to 7 research periods for Masters by Research (approximately 2 years) — subject to satisfactory annual progress
  • Access to University of Sydney research facilities, the Fisher Library system, and research support services
  • Eligibility for supplementary grants and travel funding through faculty research programs

Deadlines apply to your full application — including supervisor confirmation, research proposal, transcripts, and referee reports. Do not leave document preparation to the final weeks. Supervisor engagement should begin 3–6 months before your target deadline.

Eligibility requirements

USYDIS is open to all international students commencing a PhD or Masters by Research. You must be an international student (not an Australian citizen or permanent resident). Key eligibility criteria:

  • Academic achievement: A Weighted Average Mark (WAM) of 80 or above in your most recent completed degree — equivalent to First Class Honours or high distinction. International grading scales are converted using Sydney's equivalency tables.
  • English proficiency: IELTS Academic 6.5 overall with no band below 6.0 for most programs. Some faculties — Medicine, Education, Law — require IELTS 7.0. Verify for your specific program.
  • Research proposal: A well-developed proposal (typically 3,000–5,000 words) covering your research question, literature context, methodology, and expected contribution. Developed in consultation with your proposed supervisor.
  • Supervisor support: A confirmed or at minimum responsive supervisor is effectively required in most faculties. Unsupported applications rarely succeed.

Always verify on the official site before applying

Stipend values, eligibility conditions, and deadline dates change annually. Verify current details at sydney.edu.au/scholarships before applying.

How to find and contact a supervisor

Supervisor support is effectively required for USYDIS. The University of Sydney's academic profile search is available at find.sydney.edu.au — use it to find researchers whose current work aligns specifically with your proposed research topic, not just your broad field.

  • 1

    Search find.sydney.edu.au by keyword

    Use specific research keywords from your proposed topic, not broad field names. Filter for academics with active recent publications — within the last 3 years is ideal.

  • 2

    Read 2–3 recent papers before contacting

    Identify an open question or direction in their work that your proposed research could address. Reference this in your email — it immediately distinguishes you from generic enquiries.

  • 3

    Send a targeted email under 300 words

    Introduce yourself briefly. Reference a specific paper and the connection to your proposed research. Attach your CV and a one-page research outline. Do not attach a full proposal at first contact.

  • 4

    Follow up once after two weeks

    A single polite follow-up is appropriate. If no response after the second contact, move to your next target — academics have many demands on their attention.

  • 5

    Target 5–8 supervisors simultaneously

    Email multiple academics with individually tailored messages. Response rates vary significantly. Starting this process 3–6 months before your target application deadline gives you adequate time to find the right match.

USYDIS offers Australia's highest research stipend. This is partly offset by Sydney's higher cost of living — shared accommodation in the Inner West suburbs near campus (Glebe, Newtown, Ultimo, Surry Hills) typically ranges from AUD 1,000–1,500 per month, higher than comparable suburbs near Monash Clayton or Melbourne Parkville. The net financial position is broadly comparable to MGRS when adjusted for living costs, but Sydney's global employer brand and inner-city location offer distinct advantages for career development.

Camperdown campus — location and living costs

The University of Sydney's main Camperdown/Darlington campus is 3km southwest of the CBD — among the most centrally located of any Group of Eight university. The campus is accessible by light rail (L2/L3 to Central Station), multiple bus routes, and bicycle from most Inner West suburbs. The surrounding area — Glebe, Newtown, Camperdown, Surry Hills — is vibrant, walkable, and has a strong student community.

Living costs are higher than outer-suburban Melbourne campuses. Shared accommodation near campus ranges from AUD 1,000–1,500 per month for a room in a share house. The university offers on-campus accommodation through Sydney University Village and a range of residential colleges. On the USYDIS stipend of AUD 42,754 per year (approximately AUD 3,563 per month), living costs in Sydney are manageable — particularly given the higher stipend relative to other scholarships.

Step-by-step application process

  • 1

    Find and contact a supervisor (3–6 months before deadline)

    Use find.sydney.edu.au. Aim for written supervisor support — an email expressing willingness to supervise is sufficient at application stage.

  • 2

    Develop your research proposal with your supervisor

    3,000–5,000 words covering research question, literature context, methodology, expected contribution, and timeline. Supervisor input during drafting is both normal and valuable.

  • 3

    Prepare supporting documents

    Certified transcripts and degree certificates, CV highlighting research outputs, IELTS or PTE score, referee contact details (2–3 academics), and any publications or conference papers.

  • 4

    Apply via the Sydney Courses portal

    Apply for admission and USYDIS simultaneously through the Sydney Courses portal — there is no separate scholarship application. Target the September deadline for an early 2027 start or December for a mid-2027 start.

  • 5

    Accept offer and apply for student visa

    Outcomes are typically communicated within 8–12 weeks. Once you receive a combined admission and scholarship offer, apply for your Subclass 500 student visa through the Department of Home Affairs.
